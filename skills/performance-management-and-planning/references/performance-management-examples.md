# Performance Management - Detailed Examples

## Output-Oriented Management Examples

### Administrative Work as Production
A billing clerk:
- **Input**: Customer orders and pricing data
- **Process**: Data entry, calculation, verification
- **Output**: Accurate, timely invoices
- **Production Principles**: Standardize process, measure error rates, optimize workflow

### Creative Work as Production
A software designer:
- **Input**: Requirements and constraints
- **Process**: Design, coding, testing
- **Output**: Working software that meets requirements
- **Production Principles**: Version control, code reviews, automated testing

### Managerial Work as Production
A team manager:
- **Input**: Team members and organizational goals
- **Process**: Planning, coordinating, coaching
- **Output**: Team's completed work and results
- **Production Principles**: Clear objectives, regular feedback, process optimization

## Performance Diagnosis Examples

### Example 1: Can't Do (Capability Issue)
**Situation**: A sales representative is not meeting quota because they don't know how to use the CRM system effectively.

**Diagnosis**: Can't do - lacks capability

**Intervention**: 
- Provide CRM training
- Assign a mentor
- Create a reference guide
- Practice with mock scenarios

**Expected Outcome**: After training, performance improves

### Example 2: Won't Do (Motivation Issue)
**Situation**: A sales representative knows how to use the CRM and has strong skills, but consistently fails to enter data because they see it as "administrative busywork."

**Diagnosis**: Won't do - lacks motivation

**Intervention**:
- Explain the business value of CRM data
- Connect CRM usage to commission calculations
- Set clear expectations and consequences
- Recognize and reward compliance

**Expected Outcome**: After motivation intervention, compliance improves

## Planning Energy Examples

### Software Development Project
**Early Energy Investment (10x ROI)**:
- Thorough requirements gathering
- Architectural design
- Technology selection
- Risk assessment
- Prototype development

**Late Energy Investment (-10x ROI)**:
- Rushed bug fixes before release
- Last-minute feature changes
- Emergency patches in production
- Rework due to poor design

### Product Launch
**Early Energy Investment (10x ROI)**:
- Market research
- Customer interviews
- Competitive analysis
- Value proposition design
- Go-to-market strategy

**Late Energy Investment (-10x ROI)**:
- Last-minute marketing materials
- Emergency PR for issues
- Rushed training for sales team
- Fixes to poorly designed features
