# Compensation Design Examples

## Example 1: Middle Manager Bonus Structure

**Situation**: Designing bonus for a Department Manager with $150,000 total compensation.

**Design**:
- Bonus Target: 20% of total compensation = $30,000
- 3-Factor Split:
  - Individual Performance (50%): $15,000
  - Department Objectives (30%): $9,000
  - Corporate Financial Results (20%): $6,000

**Payout Scenarios**:
| Individual Rating | Department Result | Corporate Result | Total Bonus |
|-------------------|-------------------|------------------|------------|
| Exceeds (125%) | On Target (100%) | Exceeds (120%) | $31,950 |
| Meets (100%) | On Target (100%) | On Target (100%) | $30,000 |
| Below (75%) | Missed (80%) | Below (80%) | $19,800 |

## Example 2: Senior Manager Compensation

**Situation**: VP of Engineering with $400,000 total compensation.

**Design**:
- Bonus Target: 40% of total compensation = $160,000
- 3-Factor Split:
  - Individual Performance (40%): $64,000
  - Division Objectives (40%): $64,000
  - Corporate Financial Results (20%): $32,000

**Rationale**: Higher percentage needed for feedback at this compensation level.

## Example 3: Salary Administration - Family of Curves

**Starting Point**: All new hires start at $80,000.

**Career Paths**:

**High Performer**:
| Year | Salary | Increase |
|------|--------|----------|
| 0 | $80,000 | - |
| 1 | $88,000 | 10% |
| 2 | $99,000 | 12.5% |
| 3 | $112,000 | 13.1% |
| 4 | $128,000 | 14.3% |

**Average Performer**:
| Year | Salary | Increase |
|------|--------|----------|
| 0 | $80,000 | - |
| 1 | $84,000 | 5% |
| 2 | $88,200 | 5% |
| 3 | $92,600 | 5% |
| 4 | $97,200 | 5% |

**Below Average**:
| Year | Salary | Increase |
|------|--------|----------|
| 0 | $80,000 | - |
| 1 | $81,600 | 2% |
| 2 | $83,200 | 2% |
| 3 | $84,900 | 2% |
| 4 | $86,600 | 2% |

**Note**: After 4 years, high performer earns $41,400 more than below average.
