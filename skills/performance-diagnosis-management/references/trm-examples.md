# Task-Relevant Maturity Examples

## Example 1: Seasoned Manager in New Role
- **General Maturity**: High (experienced, successful elsewhere)
- **Task**: Managing new division with unfamiliar technology
- **TRM**: Low (new content, new environment)
- **Management Need**: Close supervision, detailed instructions initially

## Example 2: Junior Engineer on Familiar Task
- **General Maturity**: Low (new to workforce)
- **Task**: Coding in language learned in university
- **TRM**: Medium (has education, limited experience)
- **Management Need**: Coaching, moderate supervision

## Example 3: Expert Consultant
- **General Maturity**: High
- **Task**: Area of deep expertise
- **TRM**: High
- **Management Need**: Autonomy, minimal supervision

## Example 4: TRM Drop Due to Pace Change
- **Normal TRM**: High for routine tasks
- **Situation**: Crisis requiring rapid decisions with incomplete information
- **TRM**: Drops to Medium (new pace, new pressure)
- **Management Need**: Increased support temporarily

## Example 5: Cross-Functional Assignment
- **Normal TRM**: High in home function
- **Task**: Leading project in unfamiliar function
- **TRM**: Low (new content, different methods)
- **Management Need**: Detailed guidance, close oversight
