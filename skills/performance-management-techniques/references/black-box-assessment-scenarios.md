# Black Box Assessment - Scenario Examples

## Scenario 1: Sales Manager

**Output Measures (70%):**
- Sales quota achievement: 110% of target
- New client acquisition: 8 new accounts
- Revenue growth: 15% increase over prior period

**Internal Measures (30%):**
- Team development: 2 reps promoted
- Pipeline health: 3x coverage in pipeline
- Team morale: Low turnover, high engagement scores

**Trade-off Analysis:**
- Strong output justifies 70/30 weighting
- Pipeline health ensures future output
- Team development critical for scalability

---

## Scenario 2: Engineering Manager

**Output Measures (40%):**
- Product releases: 2 major releases shipped
- Feature delivery: 90% of planned features completed
- Quality: Bug count below threshold

**Internal Measures (60%):**
- Technical debt reduction: Refactored core systems
- Team capability: Hired 3 senior engineers
- Knowledge sharing: Established documentation practices

**Trade-off Analysis:**
- 60/40 weighting due to strategic importance of technical foundation
- Technical debt work invisible externally but critical long-term
- Team building essential for future capacity

## Scenario 3: Startup CEO

**Output Measures (50%):**

## Scenario 4: HR Director

**Output Measures (30%):**
- Hiring: Filled 20 open positions
- Onboarding: Reduced time-to-productivity by 20%
- Compliance: 100% audit pass rate

**Internal Measures (70%):**
- Culture health: Employee satisfaction scores improved
- Talent pipeline: Succession plans for key roles
- Organization design: Restructured for agility

**Trade-off Analysis:**
- 70/30 weighting reflects HR's strategic role
- Cultural impact outweighs transactional output
- Long-term organizational health is primary value

---

## Weighting Guidelines

### When to Emphasize Output Measures (70-90%):
- Crisis situations requiring immediate results
- Individual contributor roles
- Sales and revenue-generating functions
- Short-term project assignments

### When to Emphasize Internal Measures (70-90%):
- Strategic roles building long-term capability
- Leadership development positions
- Turnaround situations requiring foundation work
- Research and innovation functions

### When to Use Balanced Weighting (40-60% each):
- Management roles with dual responsibilities
- Stable business environments
- Roles requiring both execution and development
- Senior leadership positions
