# Compensation Calculation Examples

## Example 1: Middle Manager Compensation Design

**Employee**: Marketing Manager
**Total Compensation**: $120,000

### Bonus Calculation
- Bonus Percentage: 20% of total compensation
- Bonus Amount: $24,000

### Bonus Factor Split
| Factor | Weight | Amount | Metric |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Individual Performance | 50% | $12,000 | Supervisor rating (1-5 scale) |
| Team Objective | 30% | $7,200 | Department revenue target achievement |
| Corporate Financial | 20% | $4,800 | Company profit margin |

### Total Compensation
- Base Salary: $96,000
- Performance Bonus: $24,000
- **Total**: $120,000

## Example 2: Senior Manager Compensation Design

**Employee**: VP of Engineering
**Total Compensation**: $300,000

### Bonus Calculation
- Bonus Percentage: 40% of total compensation
- Bonus Amount: $120,000

### Bonus Factor Split
| Factor | Weight | Amount | Metric |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Individual Performance | 40% | $48,000 | Executive rating + OKR achievement |
| Division Objective | 40% | $48,000 | Engineering department productivity metrics |
| Corporate Financial | 20% | $24,000 | Company revenue growth |

### Total Compensation
- Base Salary: $180,000
- Performance Bonus: $120,000
- **Total**: $300,000

## Example 3: Salary Administration - Family of Curves

**Starting Point**: All new hires at $80,000

**After 3 Years**:

| Employee | Performance | Salary | Reason |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| A | Exceeds consistently | $95,000 | Moved up faster |
| B | Meets consistently | $88,000 | Standard progression |
| C | Below expectations | $82,000 | Minimal increases |

**After 6 Years**:

| Employee | Performance | Salary | Reason |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| A | Exceeds consistently | $125,000 | High performer track |
| B | Meets consistently | $105,000 | Standard track |
| C | Below expectations | $90,000 | Low performer track |

## Example 4: Promotion Cycle

**Employee's Career Path**:

| Year | Role | Performance | Action |
|------|------|-------------|--------|
| 1-2 | Junior Analyst | Meets | Maintain |
| 3-4 | Junior Analyst | Exceeds | Promote to Senior Analyst |
| 4-5 | Senior Analyst | Meets | Maintain (learning new role) |
| 5-7 | Senior Analyst | Exceeds | Promote to Manager |
| 7-8 | Manager | Meets | Maintain (learning new role) |
| 8-10 | Manager | Meets | No promotion (at level of competence) |

**Alternative - Atrophy Scenario**:

| Year | Role | Performance | Action |
|------|------|-------------|--------|
| 5-7 | Senior Analyst | Exceeds | No promotion (no opening) |
| 7-8 | Senior Analyst | Meets | Atrophy - lost motivation |
| 8-10 | Senior Analyst | Meets | Stuck, disengaged |
