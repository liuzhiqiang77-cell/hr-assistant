# Substance Over Style: Practical Examples

## Leadership Presence Comparison

### Scenario 1: Team Meeting

**Style-Over Leader:**
- Wears expensive suit, perfect grooming
- Sits at head of table with arms crossed
- Uses corporate jargon and buzzwords
- Speaks in generalities and platitudes
- Focuses on appearance of leadership

**Substance-Over Leader:**
- Dressed appropriately for work (key card on belt)
- Sits with team, not above them
- Uses clear, specific language
- Speaks in concrete terms with data and examples
- Focuses on solving problems and making decisions

### Scenario 2: Crisis Management

**Style-Over Leader:**
- Holds press conference to "show leadership"
- Makes reassuring but vague statements
- Blames external factors
- Focuses on managing perceptions
- Avoids tough questions

**Substance-Over Leader:**
- Gets directly involved in problem-solving
- Provides specific, actionable information
- Takes responsibility for fixing issues
- Focuses on resolving the crisis
- Answers tough questions honestly

### Scenario 3: Communication

**Style-Over Leader:**
- Uses ghostwriter for internal communications
- Focuses on tone and polish over clarity
- Avoids controversial topics
- Maintains carefully crafted persona
- Communication feels distant and corporate

**Substance-Over Leader:**
- Writes own communications directly
- Focuses on clarity and authenticity
- Addresses difficult topics head-on
- Shows vulnerability and humanity
- Communication feels personal and genuine

## Operational Precision Examples

### Intel's "Legendary Precision":

**What It Looks Like:**
- Manufacturing facilities running at 99.9% efficiency
- Supply chain optimized to the minute
- Financial models accurate to the dollar
- Decision-making based on rigorous data analysis
- Multibillion dollar investments made with high confidence

**How It's Achieved:**
- Relentless focus on metrics and measurement
- Continuous process improvement
- Deep understanding of operations at all levels
- Willingness to confront uncomfortable truths
- Investment in systems and tools that enable precision

### Contrast with Style-Over Approach:

**Style-Over Organization:**
- Focuses on quarterly earnings presentations
- Invests in marketing over operations
- Makes decisions based on intuition and relationships
- Hides problems until they can't be concealed
- Celebrates appearances over results

**Substance-Over Organization:**
- Focuses on operational excellence
- Invests in processes and capabilities
- Makes decisions based on data and analysis
- Addresses problems early and openly
- Celebrates actual achievements

## Personal Branding: Substance vs. Style

### Style-Over Personal Brand:

**Characteristics:**
- Carefully curated social media presence
- Professional photos in expensive settings
- Focus on titles and credentials
- Use of buzzwords and jargon
- Presentation of perfection

**Risks:**
- Perceived as inauthentic
- Difficulty building genuine trust
- Vulnerable to being exposed as superficial
- Limited influence on real work

### Substance-Over Personal Brand:

**Characteristics:**
- Shares real work and learnings
- Focuses on problems solved and value created
- Demonstrates expertise through action
- Uses clear, accessible language
- Shows vulnerability and growth

**Benefits:**
- Perceived as authentic and trustworthy
- Builds genuine influence
- Resilient to scrutiny
- Direct impact on real work

## Practical Implementation

### Daily Leadership Practices:

**Substance-Over Actions:**
- Spend time understanding the work, not just managing it
- Make decisions based on data, not intuition
- Communicate clearly and directly
- Take responsibility for outcomes
- Focus on solving real problems

**Style-Over Actions to Avoid:**
- Spending time on appearance and presentation
- Making decisions based on politics or perception
- Using jargon to sound knowledgeable
- Blaming others for problems
- Focusing on being seen as a leader

### Communication Guidelines:

**Substance-Over Communication:**
- Write your own messages
- Use simple, clear language
- Be specific and concrete
- Share both good and bad news
- Admit what you don't know

**Style-Over Communication to Avoid:**
- Using ghostwriters or PR teams
- Using corporate jargon and buzzwords
- Being vague and general
- Hiding or spinning bad news
- Pretending to have all the answers

### Decision-Making Approach:

**Substance-Over Decisions:**
- Gather relevant data and information
- Analyze options objectively
- Consider long-term implications
- Make clear, explainable decisions
- Take responsibility for outcomes

**Style-Over Decisions to Avoid:**
- Making decisions based on optics
- Following the crowd or conventional wisdom
- Focusing on short-term appearances
- Making vague, ambiguous decisions
- Avoiding accountability for results
