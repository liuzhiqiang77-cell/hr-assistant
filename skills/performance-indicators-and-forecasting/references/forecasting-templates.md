## Analysis Questions

1. **Trend Direction**: Are forecasts increasing or decreasing over time?

2. **Variance Magnitude**: How much do forecasts change month to month?

3. **Pattern Recognition**: Are there seasonal patterns in forecast revisions?

4. **Bias Detection**: Consistently over-optimistic or pessimistic?

5. **Accuracy Improvement**: Are recent forecasts closer to actuals than earlier ones?

## Administrative Workforce Forecast Template

### Step 1: Establish Activity Indicators

| Department | Primary Indicator | Secondary Indicator |
|------------|------------------|-------------------|
| AP | Vouchers processed | Error rate |
| Payroll | Paychecks issued | Corrections needed |
| Billing | Invoices generated | Days outstanding |

### Step 2: Calculate Workload per Person

```
Standard Output = Average monthly output / Average headcount
```

### Step 3: Forecast Future Needs

```
Required Headcount = Forecasted Workload / Standard Output
```

### Step 4: Plan Adjustments

| Quarter | Forecasted Workload | Current Staff | Required | Adjustment |
|---------|---------------------|--------------|----------|------------|
| Q1      | 10,000 vouchers     | 5            | 5        | 0          |
| Q2      | 12,000 vouchers     | 5            | 6        | +1         |
| Q3      | 15,000 vouchers     | 6            | 7.5      | +1.5       |
| Q4      | 11,000 vouchers     | 7.5          | 5.5      | -2         |

### Step 5: Implement
- Hire 2 months before need (accounting for ramp-up)
- Plan attrition (natural turnover reduces need for layoffs)
- Cross-train for flexibility
