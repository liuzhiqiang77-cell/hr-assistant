# Performance Assessment Weighting Scenarios

## Scenario 1: Sales Representative (90/10 - Output Focus)

**Context**: End of quarter, aggressive revenue target

**Output Measures (90%)**:
- Revenue against quota
- Number of deals closed
- New customer acquisitions

**Internal Measures (10%)**:
- CRM data accuracy
- Basic collaboration with sales engineers

**Rationale**: Short-term revenue is critical; internal contributions secondary

---

## Scenario 2: Engineering Manager (50/50 - Balanced)

**Context**: Stable product development period

**Output Measures (50%)**:
- Features shipped on schedule
- Bug reduction metrics
- System uptime

**Internal Measures (50%)**:
- Team development and mentoring
- Technical debt reduction
- Process improvements
- Team morale and retention

**Rationale**: Current delivery important, but building team capability equally valuable

---

## Scenario 3: Turnaround Manager (10/90 - Internal Focus)

**Context**: Fixing a dysfunctional team

**Output Measures (10%)**:
- Basic operational continuity
- Critical deliverables only

**Internal Measures (90%)**:
- Rebuilding team trust
- Establishing processes
- Hiring and developing talent
- Cultural transformation
- Removing toxic elements

**Rationale**: Must fix foundation before expecting high output

---

## Scenario 4: Research Scientist (30/70 - Future Focus)

**Context**: Early-stage R&D project

**Output Measures (30%)**:
- Papers published
- Patents filed
- Prototype demonstrations

**Internal Measures (70%)**:
- Knowledge building and documentation
- Collaboration across teams
- Identifying new research directions
- Mentoring junior researchers
- Building external academic relationships

**Rationale**: Long-term breakthroughs require investment in knowledge and relationships

---

## Dynamic Weighting

**Monthly Shifts**:
- Weighting can and should change based on priorities
- Communicate changes clearly to subordinates
- Document rationale for weighting decisions

**Example**: An engineering manager might shift from 50/50 to 80/20 during a critical product launch, then return to 50/50 afterward.
