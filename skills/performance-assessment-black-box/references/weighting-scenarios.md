# Black Box Assessment - Weighting Scenarios

## Scenario 1: Crisis Response (90/10 Output/Internal)

**Context**: Company facing immediate threat, quarter-end crunch, or critical project deadline.

**Rationale**: Short-term survival depends on immediate results. Long-term building is secondary.

**Output Measures (90%)**:
- Crisis resolved or mitigated
- Critical deadline met
- Immediate financial impact

**Internal Measures (10%)**:
- Did not cause lasting damage
- Maintained basic team cohesion

## Scenario 2: Balanced Performance (50/50 Output/Internal)

**Context**: Normal operations, steady-state business environment.

**Rationale**: Both current results and future capability are equally important for sustainable success.

**Output Measures (50%)**:
- Met performance targets
- Delivered on commitments
- Quality standards maintained

**Internal Measures (50%)**:
- Team development activities
- Process improvements implemented
- Future capability building

## Scenario 3: Organizational Restructuring (10/90 Output/Internal)

**Context**: Major reorganization, merger integration, or strategic pivot.

**Rationale**: Current output is less critical than building foundation for future success.

**Output Measures (10%)**:
- Maintained basic operations
- No critical failures

**Internal Measures (90%)**:
- Team stability maintained
- New processes established
- Change management effectiveness
- Future readiness achieved

## Scenario 4: New Manager Onboarding (30/70 Output/Internal)

**Context**: Manager in first 90 days of new role.

**Rationale**: Building relationships and understanding takes priority over immediate output.

**Output Measures (30%)**:
- No major disruptions
- Quick wins achieved

**Internal Measures (70%)**:
- Team relationships built
- Stakeholder trust established
- Understanding of business context
- Foundation for future performance

## Scenario 5: High-Potential Development (40/60 Output/Internal)

**Context**: Employee identified for leadership track.

**Rationale**: Current performance matters, but development is primary investment.

**Output Measures (40%)**:
- Strong performance in current role
- Meets or exceeds expectations

**Internal Measures (60%)**:
- Skill development activities
- Leadership potential demonstrated
- Cross-functional exposure
- Mentorship contributions

## Factors Influencing Weighting

1. **Organizational Phase**: Startup vs mature company
2. **Business Cycle**: Growth vs downturn
3. **Employee Tenure**: New vs established
4. **Role Level**: Individual contributor vs executive
5. **Strategic Priorities**: Innovation vs efficiency
