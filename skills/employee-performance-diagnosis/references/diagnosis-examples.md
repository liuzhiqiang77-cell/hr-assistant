# Performance Diagnosis Examples

## Example 1: Capability Issue (Can't Do)

**Situation**: Software engineer consistently delivers code with bugs

**Diagnosis Questions**:
- Do they understand the requirements? (Yes)
- Do they know the programming language? (No, new hire)
- Do they understand the codebase architecture? (No)

**Root Cause**: Capability - lacks knowledge of codebase and best practices

**Intervention**: TRAIN
- Pair programming with senior engineer
- Codebase walkthrough and documentation review
- Training on testing frameworks and debugging tools
- Code review feedback focused on learning

**Result**: Performance improved after 4 weeks of targeted training

## Example 2: Motivation Issue (Won't Do)

**Situation**: Experienced sales rep consistently below quota

**Diagnosis Questions**:
- Do they know how to sell? (Yes, top performer last year)
- Do they understand the products? (Yes)
- Do they have the skills? (Yes, proven track record)
- Are they applying the skills? (No, minimal activity)

**Root Cause**: Motivation - disengaged after commission structure change

**Intervention**: MOTIVATE
- Discuss impact of commission change on their income
- Realign incentives with performance
- Set clear activity expectations with accountability
- Address concerns about fairness of new structure

**Result**: Activity increased, quota attainment recovered

## Example 3: Mixed Issue

**Situation**: Project manager missing deadlines

**Diagnosis Questions**:
- Do they know project management methodology? (Yes, certified)
- Do they understand the tools? (Somewhat, new software)
- Are they applying what they know? (Inconsistently)

**Root Cause**: Mixed - partial capability gap with motivation component

**Intervention**: TRAIN + MOTIVATE
- TRAIN: Software training to close capability gap
- MOTIVATE: Clear expectations on deadline adherence, consequences for misses

**Result**: Capability improved, motivation clarified, performance stabilized

---

## Example 4: Misdiagnosis

**Situation**: Customer service rep low satisfaction scores

**Initial Diagnosis**: Motivation - assumed rep doesn't care

**Initial Intervention**: MOTIVATE
- Coaching on empathy
- Incentives for high satisfaction
- Performance improvement plan

**Result**: No improvement

**Revised Diagnosis**: Capability - rep lacks product knowledge to answer questions accurately

**Revised Intervention**: TRAIN
- Comprehensive product training
- Knowledge base access
- Shadowing experienced reps

**Result**: Satisfaction scores improved significantly

**Lesson**: Always verify diagnosis before committing to intervention
