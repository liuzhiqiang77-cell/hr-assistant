# Training vs Motivation: Intervention Strategies

## Training Interventions (Can't Do)

### When to Use
- Employee lacks necessary skills
- Employee doesn't know how to perform tasks
- Employee is new to role or technology
- Employee has knowledge gaps

### Specific Actions
- Provide formal training programs
- Assign mentor or coach
- Create skill development plan
- Offer job shadowing opportunities
- Provide reference materials and documentation
- Allow time for learning and practice

## Motivation Interventions (Won't Do)

### When to Use
- Employee has skills but doesn't apply them
- Employee chooses not to perform tasks
- Employee lacks engagement or commitment
- Performance is willful, not capability-based

### Specific Actions
- Clarify consequences of non-performance
- Identify and remove demotivators
- Align work with employee interests
- Provide recognition and rewards
- Address personal or professional barriers
- Set clear accountability measures
- Consider role realignment if mismatch exists

## Common Pitfalls

### Treating Training as Motivation
- Sending unmotivated employee to training won't help
- Wastes resources and time
- May increase frustration

### Treating Motivation as Training
- Trying to motivate someone who lacks skills
- Creates stress and anxiety
- Sets employee up for failure

### Mixed Cases
- Some performance issues have both components
- Address training first, then motivation
- Or address motivation, then provide training support
