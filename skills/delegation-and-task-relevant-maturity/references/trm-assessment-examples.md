# TRM Assessment Examples

## Scenario 1: Experienced Engineer in New Role

**Situation:** Senior engineer promoted to manage a team of 5.

**Analysis:** Despite general maturity and technical expertise, TRM for management tasks is **LOW**.

**Action:** High supervision frequency, clear operational values transmission, monitor early drafts of plans.

## Scenario 2: Marketing Specialist in Rapidly Changing Environment

**Situation:** Marketing specialist with 5 years experience, but industry trends shift quarterly.

**Analysis:** TRM is **VARIABLE** - drops with each significant change.

**Action:** Maintain frequent check-ins even with experienced employee due to high rate of change.

## Scenario 3: Veteran Researcher in Stable Field

**Situation:** PhD researcher with 15 years in slow-moving academic discipline.

**Analysis:** TRM is **HIGH** and stable.

**Action:** Minimal supervision, delegate independently, monitor only at major milestones.

## Scenario 4: Cross-Functional Assignment

**Situation:** Operations manager assigned to lead a cross-departmental project.

**Analysis:** High operational maturity in own domain, **LOW TRM** in unfamiliar functions.

**Action:** Establish cross-functional values early, frequent monitoring until patterns understood.
