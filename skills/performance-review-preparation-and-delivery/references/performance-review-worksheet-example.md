# Performance Review Worksheet Example

## Step 1: Brainstorming (Unfiltered List)
- Missed deadline on Q2 report
- Great presentation to client
- Always late to Monday meetings
- Helped new team member onboard
- Quality of code has improved
- Doesn't document work well
- Took initiative on automation project
- Interrupts others in meetings
- Strong analytical skills
- Resistant to feedback

## Step 2: Relationship Analysis
**Pattern 1:** Technical competence vs. communication
- Great presentation, strong analytical skills, improved code
- BUT interrupts others, resistant to feedback

**Pattern 2:** Time management and reliability
- Missed deadline, always late to meetings
- BUT helped new team member, took initiative

**Pattern 3:** Process adherence
- Doesn't document work well
- BUT quality of work is good

## Step 3: Message Formulation
**Message 1:** Strong technical performer who needs to develop collaborative communication skills
**Message 2:** Reliable on deliverables but needs to improve meeting punctuality and deadline management
**Message 3:** Excellent work quality that would benefit from better documentation practices

## Step 4: Evidence Gathering
**For Message 1:**
- Great presentation to client (positive)
- Strong analytical skills (positive)
- Interrupts others in meetings (negative)
- Resistant to feedback (negative)

**For Message 2:**
- Missed deadline on Q2 report (negative)
- Always late to Monday meetings (negative)
- Helped new team member (positive)
- Took initiative on automation (positive)

**For Message 3:**
- Quality of code has improved (positive)
- Doesn't document work well (negative)

## Step 5: Filtering
All three messages are memorable and actionable. Keep all three.
