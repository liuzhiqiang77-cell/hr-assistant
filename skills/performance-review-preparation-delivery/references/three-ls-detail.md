# The Three L's: Detailed Guidance

## Level: Being Frank
### The Challenge
- Straightforward praise = often harder than criticism
- Human tendency to soften bad news
- Temptation to "sugarcoat"

### Why Critical
- System credibility depends on honesty
- Subordinates need accurate feedback
- Mixed messages destroy trust

## Listen: Verification Techniques
### Nonverbal Cues to Monitor
- Eye contact (avoiding = not receiving)
- Body posture (defensive vs. open)
- Facial expressions (confusion, anger, acceptance)

### When to Rephrase
- Subordinate seems confused
- Body language suggests misunderstanding
- Verbal response doesn't match intent

### Why Not Tape Recorders?
- Words are means, not end
- Focus should be on understanding, not transcription
- Creates artificial formality

## Leave Yourself Out: Managing Supervisor Emotions
### Common Emotions
- Insecurity about delivering feedback
- Anxiety about conflict
- Guilt over negative assessment
- Stage fright

### The "Day in Court" Principle
- Subordinate's problems are at issue
- Supervisor's emotions are irrelevant
- Keep focus on performance, not feelings

### Handling Your Own Emotions
- Acknowledge they exist
- They may well up regardless of experience
- Do not let them affect content or delivery
