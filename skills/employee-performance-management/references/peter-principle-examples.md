# The Peter Principle: Examples

## Scenario 1: The Star Salesperson

### Current Role
- Top-performing sales representative
- Consistently exceeds quotas
- Excellent customer relationships

### Promotion Decision
- Promoted to Sales Manager based on sales performance

### Outcome
- Struggles with people management
- Cannot coach or develop team
- Fails to meet team sales targets
- Stuck at level of incompetence

### Prevention
- Assess leadership potential before promotion
- Look for evidence of mentoring, coaching, or team leadership
- Consider dual career track (individual contributor path)

## Scenario 2: The Brilliant Engineer

### Current Role
- Exceptional technical skills
- Solves complex problems
- High individual output

### Promotion Decision
- Promoted to Engineering Manager based on technical excellence

### Outcome
- Cannot delegate technical work
- Micromanages team's technical decisions
- Fails to develop team capabilities
- Becomes bottleneck for team

### Prevention
- Assess management interest and aptitude
- Look for evidence of project coordination, communication, and people skills
- Provide management training before promotion

## Scenario 3: The Effective First-Line Manager

### Current Role
- Successfully manages team of 5-10 people
- Good at day-to-day operations
- Team meets targets

### Promotion Decision
- Promoted to Department Director based on management success

### Outcome
- Cannot think strategically
- Struggles with cross-functional coordination
- Fails to develop long-term vision
- Stuck at level of incompetence

### Prevention
- Assess strategic thinking capabilities
- Look for evidence of cross-functional projects, strategic initiatives
- Provide exposure to higher-level responsibilities before promotion
