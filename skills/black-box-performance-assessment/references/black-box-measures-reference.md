# Black Box Assessment - Detailed Measures

## Output Measures Examples

**For Sales Roles**:
- Revenue generated
- New accounts acquired
- Deal closure rate
- Quota attainment percentage

**For Engineering Roles**:
- Features delivered
- Bugs resolved
- Code quality metrics
- On-time delivery

**For Management Roles**:
- Team performance metrics
- Budget adherence
- Project completion rate
- Customer satisfaction scores

---

## Internal Measures Examples

**Activities Creating Current Output**:
- Hours worked and effort level
- Quality of work produced
- Collaboration with peers
- Communication effectiveness
- Problem-solving initiative
- Adaptability to changes

**Activities Setting Stage for Future Output**:
- Mentoring and developing team members
- Succession planning
- Process documentation
- Knowledge sharing sessions
- Building team culture
- Improving team morale
- Preventing burnout
- Strategic planning contributions
- Innovation initiatives
- Risk mitigation activities

---

## Weighting Scenarios

### 90/10 (Output-Focused)
**When to Use**:
- Crisis situations requiring immediate results
- Short-term project roles
- Individual contributor roles with clear metrics
- Performance improvement plans

**Example**: Sales representative in end-of-quarter push

---

### 50/50 (Balanced)
**When to Use**:
- Standard management roles
- Ongoing performance assessment
- Professional roles with both immediate and long-term impact
- Most knowledge worker positions

**Example**: Engineering team lead

---

### 10/90 (Internal-Focused)
**When to Use**:
- Senior leadership roles
- Organizational development positions
- Roles focused on capability building
- Periods of strategic investment

**Example**: HR Director during organizational transformation

---

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Focusing Only on Output**
   - Ignores future capability building
   - Encourages short-termism
   - Damages long-term organizational health

2. **Focusing Only on Internal**
   - No accountability for results
   - "Busy work" without impact
   - Difficult to measure effectiveness

3. **Inconsistent Weighting**
   - Confusing for employees
- Perceived unfairness
- Undermines trust in assessment process

4. **Undefined Expectations**
   - Assessment becomes subjective
- Difficult to defend decisions
- Employees don't know what to aim for
