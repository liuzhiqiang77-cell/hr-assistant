# Assessment Weighting Scenarios

## Scenario 1: Crisis Management (90/10 Output/Internal)

**Context**
- Company facing immediate survival threat
- Short-term results critical

**Weighting**
- Output: 90%
- Internal: 10%

**Rationale**
- Future irrelevant if company fails now
- Immediate results paramount

## Scenario 2: Leadership Development (10/90 Output/Internal)

**Context**
- High-potential employee in development program
- Long-term leadership role targeted

**Weighting**
- Output: 10%
- Internal: 90%

**Rationale**
- Building future capability more important
- Current output less critical than growth

## Scenario 3: Steady-State Performance (50/50 Output/Internal)

**Context**
- Established employee in stable role
- Consistent performance expected

**Weighting**
- Output: 50%
- Internal: 50%

**Rationale**
- Balance between current delivery and future health
- Sustainable performance required

## Scenario 4: Turnaround Situation (70/30 Output/Internal)

**Context**
- Underperforming team or department
- Need immediate improvement

**Weighting**
- Output: 70%
- Internal: 30%

**Rationale**
- Must show quick results
- But can't ignore team health completely

## Trade-off Examples

**Trade-off 1: Speed vs. Quality**
- Employee delivered quickly but with quality issues
- Assessment: High output score, lower internal score (team impact)

**Trade-off 2: Individual vs. Team**
- Employee excelled individually but didn't develop team
- Assessment: High output score, low internal score (future capability)

**Trade-off 3: Short-term vs. Long-term**
- Employee hit targets but burned out team
- Assessment: High output score, very low internal score (sustainability)

**Trade-off 4: Innovation vs. Execution**
- Employee created innovative solutions but missed deadlines
- Assessment: Medium output score, high internal score (future value)
