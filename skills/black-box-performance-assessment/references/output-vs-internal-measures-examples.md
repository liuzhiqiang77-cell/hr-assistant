# Output vs. Internal Measures Examples

## Output Measures (Tangible)

### By Role:

**Software Engineer:**
- Features shipped
- Bugs resolved
- Code reviews completed
- Test coverage percentage
- Performance improvements delivered

**Sales Representative:**
- Revenue generated
- Quota achievement percentage
- New accounts acquired
- Deal size average
- Sales cycle length

**Product Manager:**
- Products launched on time
- User adoption rates
- Feature usage metrics
- Customer NPS improvement
- Roadmap completion percentage

**Operations Manager:**
- Cost reductions achieved
- Process efficiency gains
- SLA compliance rate
- Error reduction percentage
- Throughput improvements

**HR Business Partner:**
- Time-to-hire reduction
- Retention rate improvement
- Training completion rates
- Employee engagement score change
- Open positions filled

## Internal Measures (Qualitative)

### Activities Creating Current Period Output:

**Collaboration & Enablement:**
- Mentored junior team members
- Shared critical knowledge in team meetings
- Helped unblock colleagues' progress
- Facilitated cross-functional communication
- Provided technical guidance that prevented delays

**Problem-Solving:**
- Identified root cause of recurring issue
- Negotiated resources needed for team success
- Resolved conflicts that were impacting productivity
- Made quick decisions that kept project moving
- Adapting approach when initial plan failed

### Activities Setting Stage for Future Output:

**People Development:**
- Created career development plans for direct reports
- Conducted training sessions that upskilled team
- Identified and groomed high-potential employees
- Built succession pipeline for critical roles
- Provided coaching that improved team capability

**Team & Culture Building:**
- Established team norms that improved collaboration
- Created onboarding process that accelerates new hire productivity
- Built psychological safety that encourages innovation
- Fostered cross-team relationships that enable future collaboration
- Recognized and celebrated team achievements appropriately

**Systems & Processes:**
- Documented processes that prevent future knowledge loss
- Automated manual tasks that will save ongoing time
- Established quality standards that prevent future defects
- Built dashboards that provide visibility for better decision-making
- Created templates that accelerate future work

**Strategic Positioning:**
- Conducted market research that informed future roadmap
- Built relationships with key stakeholders for future initiatives
- Identified emerging risks and created mitigation plans
- Advocated for resources needed for future growth
- Established partnerships that enable future opportunities

## Trade-off Scenarios

### Scenario 1: High Output, Low Internal

**Situation:** Senior engineer delivered all features but didn't mentor juniors or document work.

**Assessment:**
- Output: 9/10
- Internal: 3/10
- Weighted: 75% Output / 25% Internal (crunch time priority)
- **Total:** 7.5/10
- **Trade-off:** Valued immediate delivery over knowledge transfer
- **Feedback:** "Excellent delivery results. For next quarter, we need you to document your work and start mentoring one junior engineer."

### Scenario 2: Moderate Output, High Internal

**Situation:** Team lead's direct reports performed well, but lead didn't deliver own projects due to management overhead.

**Assessment:**
- Output: 5/10
- Internal: 9/10
- Weighted: 40% Output / 60% Internal (leadership role emphasis)
- **Total:** 7.4/10
- **Trade-off:** Valued team enablement over personal output
- **Feedback:** "Your team is performing excellently due to your leadership. Need to find balance between managing and delivering your own commitments."

### Scenario 3: Low Output, Low Internal

**Situation:** Junior employee struggling with both tasks and team integration.

**Assessment:**
- Output: 3/10
- Internal: 4/10
- Weighted: 70% Output / 30% Internal (learning role)
- **Total:** 3.3/10
- **Trade-off:** Expected learning curve, but both areas need improvement
- **Feedback:** "Performance improvement plan required. Focus on completing assigned tasks and actively seeking help from team members."

### Scenario 4: High Output, High Internal

**Situation:** Star performer delivering results while also building team capability.

**Assessment:**
- Output: 9/10
- Internal: 9/10
- Weighted: 50% Output / 50% Internal (balanced expectations)
- **Total:** 9/10
- **Trade-off:** Recognized excellence in both dimensions
- **Feedback:** "Exceptional performance across all metrics. Ready for promotion/expanded responsibilities."
