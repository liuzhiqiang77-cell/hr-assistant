# Black Box Performance Assessment

## Core Concept

Treat performance assessment as evaluating a "black box" where:
- **Output measures** = What comes out of the box (tangible results)
- **Internal measures** = What happens inside the box (activities creating current and future output)

## Apply This Skill When

- Conducting performance reviews or assessments
- Evaluating employee contributions
- Determining bonuses, promotions, or development plans
- Weighing different types of work (immediate vs. long-term)
- Providing comprehensive performance feedback

## Execution Framework

### 1. Define Expectations Upfront

Before assessment begins:
- Clarify in advance what is expected from the subordinate
- Establish clear standards for both output and internal measures
- Communicate how different contributions will be weighted

**Critical:** Assessment must be against pre-defined standards, not arbitrary or retroactive criteria.

### 2. Evaluate Output Measures

Assess tangible results that can be plotted on charts:

**Examples:**
- Designs completed
- Sales quotas met or exceeded
- Production yield improvements
- Projects delivered on time/budget
- Bugs fixed, features shipped
- Revenue generated
- Customer satisfaction scores

**Characteristics:**
- Objective and measurable
- Time-bound (within the review period)
- Direct contribution to organizational goals
- Easy to communicate to stakeholders

### 3. Evaluate Internal Measures

Assess activities that:

**Create Output for Current Period:**
- Collaboration that enabled team success
- Problem-solving that unblocked others
- Knowledge shared that improved team capability
- Process improvements implemented

**Set Stage for Future Output:**
- Mentoring and developing other team members
- Building team cohesion and culture
- Establishing systems that prevent future problems
- Strategic planning and positioning
- Ensuring department health to prevent future disgruntlement

**Characteristics:**
- Often qualitative and harder to measure
- May not show immediate tangible results
- Critical for long-term organizational health
- Subjective but observable through behavior

### 4. Determine Weighting

There is no strict formulaâ€”weighting is **situational** and can shift monthly:

| **Context** | **Typical Weighting** | **Rationale** |
|-------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Crisis/Immediate delivery needed | 90% Output / 10% Internal | Results matter most |
| Building new team capability | 50% Output / 50% Internal | Future critical |
| Senior leadership role | 30% Output / 70% Internal | Legacy and team building |
| Individual contributor | 80% Output / 20% Internal | Deliverables primary |
| Turnaround situation | 70% Output / 30% Internal | Quick wins needed |

**Factors influencing weighting:**
- Organizational stage (startup vs. established)
- Role level and responsibilities
- Team maturity
- Business priorities
- Time horizon (quarter vs. year)

### 5. Conduct Trade-off Analysis

Make the assessment explicit by identifying:

**What is being traded:**
- Current output vs. future capability
- Individual contribution vs. team enablement
- Short-term results vs. long-term systems

**Why this weighting makes sense:**
- Link to organizational priorities
- Explain the context that informed the decision
- Connect to business objectives

**Document the rationale:**
- Ensure the employee understands how they were evaluated
- Create a record for future assessments
- Enable consistent application over time

## Constraints

- Difficult to apply strictly objectively to professional work
- Internal measures rely on observational judgment
- Weighting must be transparent to be perceived as fair
- Expectations must be defined before assessment period begins

## Result Template

"A balanced performance score of [X]/[Y] based on weighted output and internal measures, with explicit documentation of trade-offs between immediate results and future capability building."
