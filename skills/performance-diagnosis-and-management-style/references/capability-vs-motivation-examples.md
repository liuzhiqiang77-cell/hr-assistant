# Capability vs. Motivation Examples

## Capability Issues ("Can't Do It")

### Example 1: Technical Skill Gap
- **Situation**: Sales rep cannot use CRM system effectively
- **Life or Death Test**: If their life depended on it, could they use it? NO
- **Diagnosis**: Capability issue
- **Intervention**: Hands-on training on CRM system

### Example 2: Process Knowledge
- **Situation**: New hire doesn't know company approval workflow
- **Life or Death Test**: If their life depended on it, could they complete it? NO
- **Diagnosis**: Capability issue
- **Intervention**: Training on approval processes

## Motivation Issues ("Won't Do It")

### Example 1: Disengagement
- **Situation**: Experienced engineer produces minimal work, skips meetings
- **Life or Death Test**: If their life depended on it, could they perform? YES
- **Diagnosis**: Motivation issue
- **Intervention**: Motivational strategies (recognition, career path, incentives)

### Example 2: Resistance to Change
- **Situation**: Manager refuses to adopt new tools despite knowing how to use them
- **Life or Death Test**: If their life depended on it, could they use the tools? YES
- **Diagnosis**: Motivation issue
- **Intervention**: Address resistance, explain benefits, create accountability

## Task-Relevant Maturity Examples

### Immature for Specific Task
- **Employee**: Senior engineer with 10 years experience
- **Task**: Leading a cross-functional project for the first time
- **Maturity**: Immature for this specific task
- **Management Style**: Hands-on training on project management

### Mature for Specific Task
- **Employee**: Junior developer
- **Task**: Writing code in their primary language
- **Maturity**: Mature for this specific task
- **Management Style**: Delegate approach, hands-off
