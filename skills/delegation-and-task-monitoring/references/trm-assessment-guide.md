# Task-Relevant Maturity Assessment Guide

## Assessment Factors

### Achievement Orientation
- How driven is the subordinate to achieve results?
- Do they set ambitious goals?
- Do they persist through obstacles?

### Readiness for Responsibility
- Willingness to be accountable for outcomes
- History of owning mistakes and learning
- Proactive vs. reactive behavior

### Education and Training
- Relevant formal education
- Specific training for the task
- Certifications or credentials

### Experience
- Direct experience with similar tasks
- Years in relevant roles
- Track record of success

## TRM Levels

### Low TRM
- New to the task or environment
- Requires close supervision
- Needs detailed instructions
- High monitoring frequency required

### Medium/Growing TRM
- Some experience with the task
- Can work with moderate supervision
- Needs guidance on complex issues
- Moderate monitoring frequency

### High TRM
- Experienced with the task
- Works independently
- Requires minimal supervision
- Low monitoring frequency (spot checks)
