# Maslow's Need Levels - Detailed Reference

## Physiological Needs
- **Definition**: Basic necessities of life (food, clothing, shelter)
- **Driver**: Fear of deprivation/starvation
- **Example**: Working minimum wage to avoid starvation
- **Characteristics**: 
  - Most fundamental level
  - Money is viewed as utilitarian
  - Motivation extinguishes when basic needs are met

## Safety/Security Needs
- **Definition**: Protection from slipping back to deprivation
- **Driver**: Protection against catastrophic loss
- **Example**: Buying medical insurance, saving for emergencies
- **Characteristics**:
  - Rarely dominant but absence affects performance
  - Focus on stability and predictability
  - Includes job security, benefits, safe work environment

## Social/Affiliation Needs
- **Definition**: Desire to belong to a group with common traits
- **Driver**: Companionship and team identity
- **Example**: Taking lower-paying job for great colleagues
- **Characteristics**:
  - "Misery loves miserable company" - like seeks like
  - Team cohesion is powerful motivator
  - Can override financial considerations

## Esteem/Recognition Needs
- **Definition**: Desire to keep up with or emulate a specific reference group
- **Driver**: Recognition from respected peers
- **Example**: Athlete greeting a star player, executive seeking promotion
- **Characteristics**:
  - "Esteem exists in the eyes of the beholder"
  - Self-limiting: once goal reached, motivation drops
  - Money becomes a status symbol/scorecard

## Self-Actualization
- **Definition**: Need to achieve one's "utter personal best"
- **Driver**: Internal drive for excellence and growth
- **Example**: Scientist pursuing breakthrough, artist perfecting craft
- **Characteristics**:
  - Does NOT extinguish when fulfilled
  - Drives performance to ever higher levels
  - Money is purely a measure of achievement
  - Two subtypes: Competence-driven (mastery) and Achievement-driven (testing limits)

## Edge Case: Regression
In crisis situations (earthquake, layoff, economic collapse), individuals can instantly regress from high-level needs to fundamental survival needs.
