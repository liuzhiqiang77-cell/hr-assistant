# Detailed Need Level Examples

## Physiological Needs
- **Definition**: Basic necessities of life (food, clothing) that money can buy
- **Driver**: Fear of deprivation (e.g., fear of starvation)
- **Example**: Dickens-era workers toiling to avoid starvation
- **Behavior**: Will quit once monetary target is met (e.g., Caribbean plant workers)

## Safety/Security Needs
- **Definition**: Desire to protect oneself from slipping back to deprivation
- **Driver**: Protection against catastrophic loss
- **Example**: Medical insurance protecting against bankruptcy from hospital fees
- **Note**: Rarely dominant, but absence badly affects performance

## Social/Affiliation Needs
- **Definition**: Inherent desire to belong to a group with common traits
- **Driver**: Companionship and team identity
- **Example**: Taking a low-paying job solely for the companionship of colleagues
- **Quote**: "Misery loves not just any company, but the company of other miserable people"

## Esteem/Recognition Needs
- **Definition**: Desire to keep up with or emulate a specific person or group
- **Driver**: Recognition from a specific reference group
- **Example**: An athlete greeting a top player feels great to the athlete, but means nothing to family
- **Limitation**: Self-limiting. Once a goal is reached (e.g., becoming VP), the need loses urgency and stops motivating

## Self-Actualization Needs
- **Definition**: Need to achieve one's "utter personal best" in a chosen field
- **Unique Characteristic**: Does not extinguish after fulfillment; drives performance to ever higher levels
- **Types**:
  - **Competence-Driven**: Job/task mastery, sharpening skills (e.g., violinist practicing daily)
  - **Achievement-Driven**: Testing limits of capability (e.g., teenager perfecting skateboard trick)

## Behavioral Classification (Peg/Ring Experiment)
- **Gamblers**: High risk, no influence on outcome
- **Conservatives**: Very little risk
- **Achievers**: Challenge themselves at the boundary of capability to test limits
