# TRM Assessment Examples

## Example 1: High General Maturity, Low Task-Specific TRM

**Person**: Senior Marketing Director, 15 years experience, MBA, highly accomplished

**Task**: Managing the company's first cryptocurrency marketing campaign

**Assessment**:
- General maturity: Very high
- Task-specific experience: None (never worked with crypto)
- Environment: Highly volatile, rapidly changing
- **TRM Level**: Low

**Management approach**: Close supervision, frequent check-ins, detailed guidance despite senior's general experience

## Example 2: Same Person, Different Task

**Person**: Same Senior Marketing Director

**Task**: Managing the annual product launch campaign (done this 10 times)

**Assessment**:
- General maturity: Very high
- Task-specific experience: Extensive
- Environment: Familiar, predictable
- **TRM Level**: High

**Management approach**: Minimal supervision, outcome-based monitoring, full autonomy

## Example 3: TRM Shift Due to Pace Change

**Person**: Software Engineer, 3 years experience with the codebase

**Task**: Normal development work
- **TRM**: High (autonomous, minimal supervision)

**Task changes**: Critical production outage, system crashing, customer impact escalating
- **TRM drops**: Low (high pressure, unfamiliar crisis mode, pace accelerated)

**Management approach**: Shift from hands-off to close supervision during crisis

## Example 4: TRM Assessment for Meeting Frequency

**Subordinate A**: New hire, first job
- **TRM**: Low across all tasks
- **Meeting frequency**: Weekly

**Subordinate B**: 5-year veteran, but recently promoted to management
- **TRM for technical work**: High
- **TRM for management tasks**: Low
- **Meeting frequency**: Weekly (focus on management coaching)

**Subordinate C**: 10-year veteran, stable role in research department
- **TRM**: High
- **Rate of change**: Low
- **Meeting frequency**: Monthly

**Subordinate D**: 5-year veteran in marketing department
- **TRM**: High
- **Rate of change**: High (rapidly evolving market)
- **Meeting frequency**: Bi-weekly (to stay current on developments)
