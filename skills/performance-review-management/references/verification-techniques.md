# Verification Techniques

## Verbal Indicators of Understanding

### Positive Signs
- Paraphrasing: "So what you're saying is..."
- Asking clarifying questions
- Acknowledging statements: "I see," "Understood"
- Summarizing key points

### Negative Signs
- Silence without engagement
- Defensive responses: "But I..."
- Changing the subject
- Agreeing without comprehension

## Nonverbal Indicators of Understanding

### Positive Body Language
- Nodding at appropriate times
- Maintaining eye contact
- Leaning forward slightly
- Taking notes
- Relaxed posture

### Negative Body Language
- Avoiding eye contact
- Crossed arms
- Looking away frequently
- Fidgeting
- Blank expression
- Checking watch or phone

## Emotional State Assessment

### Signs of Being Overwhelmed
- Tearing up
- Voice trembling
- Rapid breathing
- Frozen expression

### Signs of Defensiveness
- Interrupting frequently
- Raising voice
- Blaming others
- Making excuses

### Signs of Disengagement
- Staring into space
- Slumped posture
- Yawning
- Checking phone

## Adjustment Strategies

### If Message Not Understood
1. Pause and ask: "Does that make sense?"
2. Rephrase using different words
3. Use a specific example
4. Ask them to repeat back what they heard
5. Break into smaller pieces

### If Emotional Reaction Occurs
1. Acknowledge the emotion: "I can see this is difficult to hear"
2. Pause to allow processing
3. Reaffirm the purpose: improvement
4. Return to the message when ready

### If Defensive Reaction Occurs
1. Don't argue or debate
2. Stick to facts and examples
3. Focus on behavior, not person
4. Ask for their perspective
