# Three L's Verification Techniques

## Verification Checklist

### Level (Frankness)
- [ ] Am I being totally honest?
- [ ] Am I holding back any criticism?
- [ ] Am I holding back any praise?
- [ ] Am I using clear, direct language?
- [ ] Am I avoiding euphemisms?

### Listen (Understanding)
- [ ] Am I making eye contact?
- [ ] Is the subordinate making eye contact?
- [ ] Are they nodding or showing understanding?
- [ ] Are they asking clarifying questions?
- [ ] Is their body language open or closed?
- [ ] Do they seem emotional (angry, sad, defensive)?
- [ ] Are they preoccupied (formulating responses)?
- [ ] Do they seem tuned out (looking away, blank stare)?

### Leave Yourself Out (Focus)
- [ ] Am I talking about my feelings?
- [ ] Am I making this about me?
- [ ] Am I feeling guilty or anxious?
- [ ] Am I apologizing for giving feedback?
- [ ] Am I defending my decisions?
- [ ] Is the focus on the subordinate's performance?

## Nonverbal Cues to Monitor

### Signs of Understanding
- Nodding
- Taking notes
- Leaning forward
- Asking relevant questions
- Paraphrasing back to you
- Appropriate emotional reactions

### Signs of Confusion
- Furrowed brow
- Squinting
- Tilting head
- Asking "what do you mean?"
- Silence
- Looking away

### Signs of Defensiveness
- Crossed arms
- Leaning back
- Interrupting
- Making excuses
- Blaming others
- Angry facial expressions

### Signs of Disengagement
- Blank stare
- Looking at watch/phone
- Yawning
- Slouching
- No questions
- Minimal responses

## Adjustment Strategies

### If Confused
- Explain again in different words
- Use specific examples
- Ask what's unclear
- Draw a diagram if helpful
- Check understanding: "What's your take on this?"

### If Defensive
- Acknowledge their feelings
- Focus on behavior, not person
- Remind them of purpose (improvement)
- Give them time to process
- Return to specific examples

### If Disengaged
- Take a break
- Ask direct question
- Check if something else is on their mind
- Reschedule if necessary
- Ask what would help them engage

### If Emotional
- Pause and let them process
- Acknowledge the emotion
- Don't argue with feelings
- Return to facts when ready
- Offer to continue later

## Common Mistakes

### Level Mistakes
- Being too harsh without context
- Being too soft to avoid conflict
- Using sandwich technique (dilutes message)
- Focusing only on negatives or only on positives

### Listen Mistakes
- Talking too much without checking understanding
- Assuming silence means agreement
- Not watching nonverbal cues
- Moving on too quickly
- Using tape recorder (focuses on words, not understanding)

### Leave Yourself Out Mistakes
- Making it about your discomfort
- Apologizing for feedback
- Defending your decisions
- Sharing your own problems
- Making subordinate responsible for your feelings
