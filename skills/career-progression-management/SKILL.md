# Career Progression Management

Manage the full lifecycle of career progression: compensation design, promotion decisions, and recovery from failed promotions.

## When to Use
- Designing or reviewing compensation structures for middle/senior managers
- Deciding whether to promote an employee
- Managing an employee who is struggling after a promotion
- Determining bonus percentages and base pay strategies

---

## Component 1: Compensation System Design

### Design Performance Bonus (Variable Pay)

#### Step 1: Determine Bonus Percentage

| Role | Bonus as % of Total Compensation |
|------|--------------------------------|
| Middle Manager | 10% - 25% |
| Senior Manager | Up to 50% |

**Rationale**: Higher earners have less material utility for incremental dollars; higher percentage needed for feedback while avoiding hardship from fluctuations.

#### Step 2: Define Bonus Factors

Split bonus among factors based on performance scope:

| Factor | Weight | Example Metric |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Individual Performance | Variable | Supervisor's subjective judgment |
| Immediate Team Objective | Variable | Department KPIs (e.g., revenue, customer satisfaction) |
| Corporate Financial Performance | Variable | Company profit, stock price, revenue |

**Recommended**: 3-factor split balances individual, team, and organizational alignment.

#### Step 3: Establish Timing

- Pay bonus close enough to work time so subordinate remembers why it was awarded
- Account for cause/effect time offsets (some results take time to materialize)

#### Step 4: Select Metrics

Options in order of preference:
1. **Countable items** (financial metrics, units shipped)
2. **Measurable objectives** (goals with specific targets)
3. **Subjective elements** (last resort; may lead to "beauty contest")

### Design Salary Administration (Base Pay)

#### Select Administration Scheme

| Scheme | Description | Pros | Cons |
|--------|-------------|------|------|
| Experience-Only | Pay based strictly on time (X time = Y pay) | Easy, predictable | Ignores merit, demotivates high performers |
| Merit-Only | Pay based on performance only | Fair, motivating | Hard to ignore experience, difficult to implement |
| Compromise (Recommended) | "Family of curves" - starts together, different rates | Balances experience and merit | Requires competitive evaluation |

**Recommended Approach (Family of Curves)**:
- Start everyone at same salary level
- Individuals move up at different speeds based on performance
- Shape approximates experience-only curve but allows variance

#### Implement Competitive Ranking (Required for Merit)

**Principle**: If someone is first, someone else must be last (similar to sports rankings).

Accept that merit-based systems require competitive, comparative evaluation.

## Component 2: Promotion Performance Cycle

### The Promotion Logic

```
1. Initial State: Employee at Job 1, performing at "meets requirements" (average)

2. Improvement Phase: With training and motivation, performance improves to
   "exceeds requirements" (above average)

3. Promotion Trigger: Once "exceeds requirements", employee is "promotable"

4. Promotion: Employee promoted to Job 2

5. Performance Reset: In Job 2, employee initially performs at "meets requirements"
   due to new challenges

6. Cycle Repetition: With experience, improves to "exceeds requirements" â†’ another promotion

7. Cycle Conclusion: Employee settles at "meets requirements" and is no longer promoted
   (reaching their "level of incompetence")
```

### Critical Decision Rule

```
IF Employee Performance Rating = "Exceeds Requirements":
    THEN Evaluate for Promotion
    
IF Employee Performance Rating = "Meets Requirements":
    THEN Maintain Current Level
    
CRITICAL CONSTRAINT:
    IF Employee "Exceeds Requirements" AND is NOT promoted:
        THEN Employee will atrophy and performance permanently returns to "Meets Requirements"
```

### Promotion Decision Framework

| Condition | Action | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Consistently exceeds requirements | Promote | Employee has mastered current role and needs new challenges |
| Meets requirements | Maintain | Employee is appropriately placed |
| Below requirements | Develop or remove | Performance improvement required before promotion consideration |
