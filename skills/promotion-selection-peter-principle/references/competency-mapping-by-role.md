# Competency Differences by Role Level

## Individual Contributor to Manager Transition

### Individual Contributor Skills (Current Role)
- Technical expertise in specific domain
- Task execution and completion
- Personal productivity and time management
- Problem-solving within scope
- Following established processes

### First-Line Manager Skills (Intended Role)
- Delegation and task assignment
- Performance feedback and coaching
- Team coordination and communication
- Planning and resource allocation
- Conflict resolution
- Hiring and developing talent

### Gap to Address
- From doing work to getting work done through others
- From individual success to team success
- From technical focus to people focus
- From self-management to team management

## Manager to Senior Manager Transition

### Manager Skills (Current Role)
- Managing a team of individual contributors
- Operational execution and delivery
- Day-to-day problem-solving
- Direct supervision and guidance

### Senior Manager Skills (Intended Role)
- Managing other managers
- Strategic thinking and planning
- Cross-functional coordination
- Organizational design and change
- Developing managers as leaders
- Broader business understanding

### Gap to Address
- From operational to strategic thinking
- From direct team to multi-team coordination
- From solving problems to preventing problems
- From managing people to developing leaders

## Senior Manager to Executive Transition

### Senior Manager Skills (Current Role)
- Managing a department or function
- Functional expertise and depth
- Multi-team coordination
- Operational excellence within function

### Executive Skills (Intended Role)
- Enterprise-wide perspective
- Industry and market understanding
- Strategic vision and direction
- Organizational culture and values
- Stakeholder management (board, investors, public)
- Long-term decision-making under uncertainty

### Gap to Address
- From functional to enterprise perspective
- From operational to strategic time horizons
- From internal to external focus
- From managing to leading

# Assessment Methods for Promotion Decisions

## Before Promotion: Assessment

### Competency Interviews
- Behavioral questions about future role scenarios
- "Tell me about a time you..." focused on target role skills
- Assessment of potential, not just past performance

### Simulation Exercises
- In-basket exercises with managerial decisions
- Role-playing difficult conversations
- Case studies requiring strategic thinking
- Presentation to simulated stakeholders

### 360-Degree Feedback
- Input from peers, subordinates, and managers
- Leadership potential assessments
- Feedback on gaps that need development

### Trial Assignments
- Acting role for a period
- Leading a special project
- Interim responsibilities
- Low-risk testing of new skills

## After Promotion: Support

### First 90 Days Plan
- Clear expectations and success metrics
- Regular check-ins and feedback
- Mentorship from experienced leader
- Training on new skills needed

### Ongoing Development
- Leadership development programs
- Executive coaching
- Peer learning groups
- Continuous feedback mechanisms

### Safety Nets
- Clear criteria for success
- Defined support resources
- Willingness to revert if not working
- No stigma in "returning" to previous role
