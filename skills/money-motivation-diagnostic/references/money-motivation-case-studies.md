# Money Motivation Case Studies

## Case 1: Caribbean Plant Workers (Physiological Need)

**Scenario**: Workers given a specific monetary target to earn

**Reaction**: Focused on absolute sum needed to reach target

**Outcome**: Workers quit immediately after reaching the target

**Analysis**: Money satisfied a deficit need (physiological). Once the need was met, motivation ceased.

## Case 2: Venture Capitalist (Self-Actualization)

**Scenario**: VC earns first $10 million, then pursues second $10 million

**Reaction**: Focus on relative comparison and achievement scorecard

**Outcome**: Motivation continues; second $10 million is just as important as first

**Analysis**: Money serves as measure of achievement. Motivation is boundless.

## Case 3: Middle Manager (Esteem Need)

**Scenario**: Manager receives 5% raise but learns peer received 7%

**Reaction**: Dissatisfied despite absolute increase; focused on relative comparison

**Outcome**: Motivation affected by perceived status relative to peers

**Analysis**: Money is recognition from reference group. Relative ranking matters more than absolute amount.

## Case 4: Entry-Level Employee (Safety Need)

**Scenario**: Employee receives raise that covers rent increase

**Reaction**: Focused on absolute amount covering basic expenses

**Outcome**: Satisfied once financial security is achieved

**Analysis**: Money satisfies safety/security need. Motivation tied to maintaining standard of living.
