# Self-Actualization Driver Types

## Competence-Driven
**Focus**: Job or task mastery

**Behaviors**:
- Sharpening own skills continuously
- Trying to do better than the time before
- Focused on personal improvement

**Examples**:
- A violinist practicing daily
- A teenager perfecting a skateboard trick
- A coder refactoring code for elegance

**Management Approach**:
- Provide opportunities for skill development
- Set personal best challenges
- Recognize mastery milestones

---

## Achievement-Driven
**Focus**: Abstract need to achieve in all endeavors

**Behaviors**:
- Testing limits of capability
- Working at the "boundary of their capability"
- Seeking challenges at edge of ability

**Behavioral Classification (Peg/Ring Experiment)**:

**Gamblers**:
- High risk
- No influence on outcome
- Not true achievers

**Conservatives**:
- Very little risk
- Stay within comfort zone
- Not testing limits

**Achievers**:
- Challenge themselves at boundary of capability
- 50% success rate is optimal
- Test limits intentionally

**Management Approach**:
- Set stretch goals with 50% success probability
- Provide challenging assignments
- Recognize achievement, not just success
- Normalize failure as part of growth

---

## Commonality
Both types spontaneously try to test the outer limits of their abilities. The key management task is to provide the right challenges and environment for this natural drive to flourish.
