# Detailed Examples of Maslow's Need Levels

## Physiological Needs Examples

**Historical Context:**
- Dickens' era workers: Motivated by fear of starvation
- Caribbean plant workers: Quit after reaching specific monetary target

**Modern Indicators:**
- Employee focuses on absolute salary amount
- Motivation tied to meeting basic living expenses
- Performance drops once financial target achieved

## Safety/Security Needs Examples

**Insurance Behavior:**
- Medical insurance purchases to protect against bankruptcy
- Job security concerns override growth opportunities
- Preference for stable, predictable income over high-risk/high-reward

**Organizational Signs:**
- Resistance to change or restructuring
- Emphasis on tenure and seniority
- Risk-averse decision making

## Social/Affiliation Needs Examples

**Team Dynamics:**
- "Misery loves not just any company, but the company of other miserable people"
- Taking lower-paying job for colleague companionship
- Excited people seek other excited people

**Workplace Indicators:**
- Strong identification with team or department
- Social events highly valued
- Peer approval matters more than manager recognition

## Esteem/Recognition Needs Examples

**Reference Group Behavior:**
- "Keeping up with the Joneses" phenomenon
- Athlete greeting top player feels great to athlete, meaningless to family
- Status symbols important only within specific reference group

**Self-Limiting Nature:**
- Once VP goal achieved, need loses urgency
- Continuous escalation of reference group standards
- Motivation tied to external validation

## Self-Actualization Examples

**Competence-Driven:**
- Violinist practicing daily for mastery
- Teenager perfecting skateboard trick
- Craftsman refining technique

**Achievement-Driven:**
- Venture Capitalists: "Second ten million just as important as first"
- Peg/ring experiment: Achievers choose 50% success probability
- Testing boundaries of capability

**Behavioral Classification:**
- Gamblers: High risk, no influence on outcome
- Conservatives: Very little risk
- Achievers: Challenge at boundary of capability
