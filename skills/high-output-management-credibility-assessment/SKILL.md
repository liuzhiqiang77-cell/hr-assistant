# High Output Management Credibility Assessment

Evaluate the credibility and practical foundation of management principles from High Output Management.

## When to Use
- Assessing the validity or source of management principles
- Understanding the practical vs. theoretical basis of the framework
- Explaining the background of Intel's management practices
- Evaluating the author's credentials and experience

## Procedure

1. **Identify Source of Ideas**
   - Recognize that principles are derived from collective effort with Intel managers
   - Understand the collaborative development process involving many managers over years

2. **Verify Author's Learning Path**
   - Note that Andrew S. Grove learned management from Intel managers
   - Acknowledge the practical, experience-based nature of the knowledge

3. **Identify Key Contributors**
   - Gordon Moore: Intel founder who recognized Grove's managerial potential
   - Middle Managers: Provided real-world examples and acted as "guinea pigs" for early articulation

4. **Review Author's Credentials**
   - Intel co-founder and former CEO/President
   - Time magazine's Man of the Year (1997)
   - Stanford Graduate School of Business professor (24 years)
   - Practical industry experience from 1956 immigration through 2004 retirement

5. **Consider Editorial Process**
   - Ideas were translated from "engineeringese" to English by professional editor
   - Content was refined for clarity and accessibility

## Key Insights
- The framework is based on practical experience, not just theory
- Ideas were tested and refined through real-world application at Intel
- Author has extensive practical leadership experience
