# Intel's Production Model

## The 25/25/25/25 Framework

Intel categorized all organizational work into four production roles:

### 1. Make the Products (25%)
**Direct Production Workers**
- Manufacturing operators
- Assembly line workers
- Fabrication technicians
- **Output**: Physical products or components

### 2. Support Production (25%)
**Production Support Roles**
- Supervisors
- Maintenance technicians
- Manufacturing engineers
- Quality control specialists
- **Output**: Enabled and maintained production capacity

### 3. Work in Administration (25%)
**Administrative Production Roles**
- Schedulers
- Billing clerks
- Payroll specialists
- Procurement staff
- **Output**: Administrative deliverables (schedules, bills, payments)

### 4. Design, Sell, and Service Products (25%)
**Market-Facing Production Roles**
- Product designers
- Sales representatives
- Customer service representatives
- Marketing staff
- **Output**: Product designs, sales, service delivery

## Key Insight

Every role in the organization can be viewed as "producing" something:
- Even administrative work produces documents, schedules, transactions
- Design work produces specifications and plans
- Sales work produces orders and customer relationships
- Service work produces satisfied customers

This framing allows:
- Application of manufacturing discipline to all work
- Measurement of output across all functions
- Systematic approach to productivity improvement
- Common language for discussing organizational performance
