# Performance Diagnosis Toolkit

## Capability Assessment (Can't Do)

### Diagnostic Questions
- Does the employee have the necessary skills?
- Have they received adequate training?
- Do they understand the expectations?
- Do they have the required resources?
- Is the task within their ability?

### Training Interventions
- On-the-job training
- Formal coursework
- Mentoring/coaching
- Job rotation
- Skills assessment and gap analysis

## Motivation Assessment (Won't Do)

### Diagnostic Questions
- Does the employee understand why the task matters?
- Are they rewarded for performance?
- Do they have conflicting priorities?
- Is there a personal issue affecting engagement?
- Do they feel valued and recognized?

### Motivation Interventions
- Clarify impact and importance
- Adjust rewards and recognition
- Remove obstacles and friction
- Provide autonomy and ownership
- Address personal or team issues

## Promotion Decision Framework

### Before Promotion
1. Define competencies required for new role
2. Assess candidate against new role requirements
3. Identify gaps between current and required skills
4. Create development plan to address gaps
5. Use trial period to test fit

### Promotion Criteria
- Demonstrated skills for NEW role (not just current role)
- Leadership potential for next level
- Track record of developing others
- Strategic thinking ability
- Cross-functional collaboration skills

## Product Planning Phase Energy Allocation

### Phase 1: Concept (Highest Energy - 10x ROI)
- Market research
- Requirements definition
- Feasibility analysis
- Risk assessment

### Phase 2: Design (High Energy - 5x ROI)
- Architecture
- Prototyping
- Technical specifications
- Early testing

### Phase 3: Development (Medium Energy - 2x ROI)
- Implementation
- Integration
- Regular testing
- Progress monitoring

### Phase 4: Launch (Low Energy - 1x ROI)
- Deployment
- Training
- Documentation
- Initial support

### Phase 5: Maintenance (Negative Energy - -10x ROI)
- Bug fixes
- Emergency patches
- Workarounds
- Rework
