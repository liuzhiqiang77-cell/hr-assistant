# Salary Administration Curve Examples

## Experience-Only Curve (Option A)

```
Salary ($)
     ↑
 80k │                    ••••••••••••••••••••
     │                 •••
 60k │              •••
     │           •••
 40k │        •••
     │     •••
 20k │  •••
     │•••
     └────────────────────────────→ Years of Experience
       0   2   4   6   8   10  12  14
```

**Characteristics:**
- Everyone follows same path
- No variance for performance
- Predictable budgeting
- No performance incentive

## Merit-Only Curve (Option B)

```
Salary ($)
     ↑
100k │              • (Top Performer)
     │
 80k │         ••••• (Above Average)
     │      ••
 60k │   •••••••••• (Average)
     │ ••
 40k │• (Below Average)
     │
 20k │
     └────────────────────────────→ Years of Experience
       0   2   4   6   8   10  12  14
```

**Characteristics:**
- High variance based on performance
- Strong performance incentive
- Difficult to administer
- May ignore experience value
- Budget unpredictability

## Compromise "Family of Curves" (Option C - Recommended)

```
Salary ($)
     ↑
100k │                    •••••••••••••••••••• (Top Performer Curve)
     │                 •••
 80k │              •••••••••••••••••••• (Above Average Curve)
     │           ••••••
 60k │        •••••••••••••••••••• (Average Curve)
     │     ••••••••
 40k │  •••••••••••••••••••• (Below Average Curve)
     │ ••••••••
 20k │••••••••••••••••••••
     └────────────────────────────→ Years of Experience
       0   2   4   6   8   10  12  14
```

**Characteristics:**
- Multiple curves based on performance trajectory
- Starts everyone at same point
- Allows variance based on sustained performance
- Balances experience and merit
- Manageable budgeting
- Clear advancement paths
