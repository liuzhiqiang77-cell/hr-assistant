# Performance Diagnosis Scenarios

## Scenario 1: Salesperson Missing Quota

**Situation**: Salesperson consistently below target

**Life or Death Test**: "If their life depended on making sales, could they do it?"
- **Answer**: YES - They have made sales before, have product knowledge
- **Diagnosis**: Won't do it (Motivation issue)
- **Intervention**: 
  - Review commission structure
  - Assess if goals are achievable
  - Check for burnout
  - Provide recognition for wins

## Scenario 2: New Software Developer Struggling

**Situation**: Developer cannot complete assigned tasks despite effort

**Life or Death Test**: "If their life depended on writing this code, could they do it?"
- **Answer**: NO - Lacks specific technical skills, unfamiliar with codebase
- **Diagnosis**: Can't do it (Capability issue)
- **Intervention**:
  - Provide technical training
  - Pair programming with senior developer
  - Assign simpler tasks initially
  - Mentorship program

## Scenario 3: Experienced Manager Suddenly Underperforming

**Situation**: Manager with good track record now missing deadlines

**Life or Death Test**: "If their life depended on hitting deadlines, could they do it?"
- **Answer**: YES - Has demonstrated capability in past
- **Diagnosis**: Won't do it (Motivation issue)
- **Intervention**:
  - Explore personal issues (burnout, health, family)
  - Check if goals changed without buy-in
  - Assess if resources were reduced
  - Reconnect with purpose and impact

## Scenario 4: Factory Worker Quality Issues

**Situation**: Worker producing defective parts

**Life or Death Test**: "If their life depended on producing quality parts, could they do it?"
- **Answer**: NO - Equipment is faulty, training was inadequate
- **Diagnosis**: Can't do it (Capability issue - external factors)
- **Intervention**:
  - Fix or replace equipment
  - Provide proper training
  - Improve work instructions
  - Ensure quality tools available

## Scenario 5: Research Scientist Not Publishing

**Situation**: Scientist not producing papers despite working long hours

**Life or Death Test**: "If their life depended on publishing, could they do it?"
- **Answer**: UNCLEAR - May be capability (research skills) or motivation (fear of failure)
- **Further Investigation Needed**:
  - Review past publication record
  - Assess quality of work in progress
  - Explore fear of peer review
  - Check if research direction is viable

## Common Pitfalls

### Misdiagnosing Motivation as Capability
- **Error**: Sending motivated but struggling employee to training
- **Result**: Wasted resources, employee frustration
- **Correct Approach**: Address motivational barriers first

### Misdiagnosing Capability as Motivation
- **Error**: Applying pressure to employee who lacks skills
- **Result**: Increased stress, decreased performance, potential burnout
- **Correct Approach**: Provide training and support

### Ignoring External Factors
- **Error**: Assuming issue is always employee's capability or motivation
- **Result**: Missing systemic problems (bad equipment, poor processes)
- **Correct Approach**: Rule out external factors before diagnosing individual

### Focusing on Attitude Instead of Output
- **Error**: Trying to make employee "feel motivated"
- **Result**: Happy but unproductive employee
- **Correct Approach**: Design environment for performance, measure results
