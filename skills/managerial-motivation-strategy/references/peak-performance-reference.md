# Peak Performance Management - Detailed Reference

## Driver Types

### Competence-Driven
- **Focus**: Job or task mastery
- **Behavior**: Sharpening own skill, trying to do better than before
- **Example**: Violinist practicing daily, teenager perfecting skateboard trick
- **Management**: Provide opportunities for skill development and mastery

### Achievement-Driven
- **Focus**: Abstract need to achieve in all endeavors
- **Behavior**: Testing limits of capability, working at "boundary of capability"
- **Behavioral Classification** (peg/ring experiment):
  - *Gamblers*: High risk, no influence on outcome
  - *Conservatives*: Very little risk
  - *Achievers*: Challenge themselves at boundary of capability
- **Management**: Provide challenging goals with appropriate risk level

## MBO System Details

### Goal Difficulty Setting
- Target: 50% probability of success even with maximum effort
- Too easy: No motivation, low output
- Too hard: Demotivation, giving up
- Just right: Maximum stretch, peak performance even with failures

### Cultural Environment Types

**Knowledge-Centered Culture**
- Values abstract knowledge
- "Knowing" is esteemed over "producing"
- Results: Low actual achievement, high theoretical discussion

**Output-Centered Culture**
- Values tangible results
- Producing is esteemed over knowing
- Results: High actual achievement, practical application

## Constraints

- Assumes basic physiological and safety needs are met
- Not applicable for routine tasks requiring no creativity or stretch
- Requires manager to accept that some failure is acceptable for peak performance
