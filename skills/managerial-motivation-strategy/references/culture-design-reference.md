# Culture Design for Motivation

## Output-Centered vs Knowledge-Centered Cultures

### Knowledge-Centered Culture
**Characteristics:**
- Values abstract knowledge and expertise
- Rewards knowing things over producing results
- "I know the theory" is valued more than "I built this"

**Risks:**
- Low actual achievement
- Analysis paralysis
- Expertise without application
- Low motivation for achievers

### Output-Centered Culture
**Characteristics:**
- Values tangible results and deliverables
- Rewards production and measurable outcomes
- "I built this" is valued more than "I know the theory"

**Benefits:**
- High actual achievement
- Clear performance metrics
- Motivates achievement-driven employees
- Self-actualization through results

**Implementation:**
1. Define what "output" means for each role
2. Create visible scoreboards for results
3. Recognize and celebrate tangible achievements
4. Tie rewards to deliverables, not tenure
5. Share success stories focused on outcomes

## Fear Management

### Positive Fear (Fear of Failure)
- Spurs performance and preparation
- Drives attention to detail
- Creates competitive edge
- **Management**: Allow and channel constructively

### Negative Fear (Fear of Consequences)
- Causes conservative risk-avoidance
- Leads to hiding problems
- Reduces creativity and innovation
- **Management**: Remove through psychological safety
