# Management by Objectives (MBO) Implementation

## Goal Setting Framework

### The 50% Success Probability Rule

**Rationale**: 
- Output tends to be greater when striving for a level beyond immediate grasp
- Even with 50% failure rate, total output exceeds easy goals
- Forces individuals to work at their "boundary of capability"

**Implementation Steps**:
1. Assess individual's current capability level
2. Identify objectives just beyond current reach
3. Verify objective requires maximum effort to achieve
4. Set success probability at approximately 50%
5. Monitor progress and adjust as capability grows

**Example**:
- Current capability: 100 units/week consistently
- Easy goal: 110 units/week (90% success probability)
- Optimal MBO goal: 125 units/week (50% success probability)
- Expected outcome: Average of 115-120 units/week (higher than easy goal)

### Distinguishing Driver Types

**Competence-Driven Employees**:
- **Focus**: Mastery of specific job or task
- **Behavior**: 
  - Sharpening own skills continuously
  - Trying to do better than previous attempts
  - Deep focus on craft/technique
- **Management Approach**:
  - Provide opportunities for skill development
  - Set mastery-based goals
  - Recognize technical excellence
- **Examples**: Craftsmen, artists, technical specialists

**Achievement-Driven Employees**:
- **Focus**: Abstract need to achieve across all endeavors
- **Behavior**:
  - Testing limits of capability
  - Working at boundary of ability
  - Seeking challenges in multiple areas
- **Management Approach**:
  - Provide diverse challenges
  - Set stretch goals across domains
  - Recognize achievement broadly
- **Examples**: Entrepreneurs, high-performing executives, athletes

## Culture Design

### Output-Centered Culture Characteristics
- Tangible results are highly valued and esteemed
- Abstract knowledge without application gets little recognition
- "What have you produced?" is the key question
- Performance is visible and comparable
- Success is measured by outcomes, not intentions

### Knowledge-Centered Culture Characteristics (Avoid)
- Knowing abstractly is valued over producing results
- Credentials and expertise matter more than output
- "What do you know?" is the key question
- Theoretical understanding trumps practical application
- Success is measured by status, not results

1. Identify current cultural norms
2. Begin measuring and publicizing output metrics
