# Performance Assessment Detailed Reference

## Output Measures: Detailed Examples

### Quantitative Metrics
- Sales revenue generated
- Units produced
- Projects completed
- Customer satisfaction scores
- Budget adherence
- Error rates reduced

### Qualitative but Tangible Results
- Successful product launch
- Resolved critical client issue
- Implemented new system
- Won competitive bid

## Internal Measures: Detailed Examples

### Current Period Contributions
- Collaboration with peers
- Mentoring junior staff
- Process improvements implemented
- Knowledge sharing

### Future Capability Building
- Team development activities
- Succession planning preparation
- Organizational culture enhancement
- Innovation initiatives started

## Weighting Decision Framework

### Factors Favoring High Output Weight (e.g., 80/20 or 90/10)
- Crisis situation requiring immediate results
- Short-term project with defined end date
- Role with clear, measurable deliverables
- Performance improvement plan

### Factors Favoring High Internal Weight (e.g., 20/80 or 10/90)
- Leadership development role
- Long-term strategic position
- Investment period for new initiatives
- Turnaround situation requiring culture change

### Factors Favoring Balanced Weight (e.g., 50/50 or 60/40)
- Ongoing operational role
- Middle management position
- Stable team environment
- Normal business conditions

## Common Assessment Errors

### Over-Emphasizing Output
- Ignoring long-term capability building
- Rewarding short-termism at expense of future
- Missing valuable but less visible contributions

### Over-Emphasizing Internal Measures
- Accepting poor results because of "good effort"
- Failing to hold accountable for outcomes
- Rewarding activity rather than achievement
