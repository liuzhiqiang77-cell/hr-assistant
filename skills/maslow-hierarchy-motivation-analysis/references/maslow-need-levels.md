# Maslow's Need Levels - Detailed Reference

## Physiological Needs

**Definition**: Basic necessities of life (food, clothing, shelter) that money can buy.

**Driver**: Fear of deprivation (e.g., fear of starvation).

**Examples**:
- Working to avoid starvation (Dickens' era laborers)
- Accepting any job that provides basic sustenance
- Focus on absolute monetary amounts to meet basic needs

**Characteristics**:
- Most fundamental level
- Once satisfied, ceases to motivate
- Money is viewed as a utilitarian resource

## Safety/Security Needs

**Definition**: Desire to protect oneself from slipping back to deprivation.

**Driver**: Protection against catastrophic loss.

**Examples**:
- Medical insurance protecting against bankruptcy from hospital fees
- Job security concerns
- Savings and emergency funds

**Characteristics**:
- Rarely dominant, but absence badly affects performance
- Focus on stability and predictability
- Money is viewed as security/protection

## Social/Affiliation Needs

**Definition**: Inherent desire to belong to a group with common traits.

**Driver**: Companionship and team identity.

**Examples**:
- Taking a low-paying job solely for the companionship of colleagues
- "Misery loves not just any company, but the company of other miserable people"
- Excited people want to be with excited people

**Characteristics**:
- Focus on relationships and belonging
- Team identity is powerful
- Can override financial considerations

## Esteem/Recognition Needs

**Definition**: Desire to keep up with or emulate a specific person or group ("keeping up with the Joneses").

**Driver**: Recognition from a specific reference group.

**Examples**:
- An athlete greeting a top player (feels great to the athlete, means nothing to family)
- Seeking promotion to VP level
- Comparing salary to peers

**Characteristics**:
- Esteem exists in the eyes of the beholder
- Self-limiting: once a goal is reached, the need loses urgency
- Money becomes a measure of achievement/status
- Focus on relative comparison rather than absolute amounts

## Self-Actualization Needs

**Definition**: Need to achieve one's "utter personal best" in a chosen field.

**Driver**: Inner drive for growth and excellence.

**Examples**:
- A violinist practicing daily to improve
- A teenager perfecting a skateboard trick
- An entrepreneur building a company for the challenge

**Characteristics**:
- Does not extinguish after fulfillment
- Drives performance to ever higher levels
- Two types: Competence-driven (task mastery) and Achievement-driven (testing limits)
- Money is a scorecard for success, not a need satisfier
- Motivation is boundless
