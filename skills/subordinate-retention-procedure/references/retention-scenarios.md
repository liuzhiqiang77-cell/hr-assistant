# Retention Scenarios and Examples

## When NOT to Use This Procedure
- Employee received 50% salary increase and that's the only reason
- Employee is moving to be closer to family (personal reasons)
- Employee has been underperforming despite coaching
- Employee is actively toxic to team culture

## Sample Opening Conversation

**Manager:** "John, I understand you're resigning. Before we go any further, I need to understand why. Can you tell me what's going on?"

**John:** (Gives rehearsed reasons about wanting new challenges)

**Manager:** "I hear you on the challenges. But is there something else? Have you been feeling undervalued or that your work hasn't been recognized?"

**John:** "Well, honestly, I put in 80-hour weeks on Project X and nobody said a word..."

## Solution Options to Consider
- Role expansion with new responsibilities
- Transfer to different department/manager
- Promotion opportunity
- Special project assignment
- Increased visibility to leadership
- Training and development investment
