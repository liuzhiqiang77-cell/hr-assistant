# Delegation and Monitoring Workflow

Effective delegation requires shared values, appropriate task selection based on maturity, and monitoring that ensures quality without becoming micromanagement.

## When to Use

- Freeing up manager time for higher-leverage activities
- Developing subordinate capabilities
- Assigning work that matches subordinate maturity level
- Ensuring delegated work meets expectations

## Establish Prerequisites

### Shared Operational Values

Before delegating, ensure commonality of operational values, priorities, and preferences:

1. **Establish Commonality**: Shared understanding of how the organization works together is essential for delegation.
2. **Internalize Structure**: As TRM grows, structure moves from externally imposed to internally adopted.
3. **Supervisor Teaching Duty**: The responsibility for transmitting values rests with the supervisor.
4. **Prevent Customer Tuition**: Do not allow subordinates to learn through customer-impacting mistakes. Teach correct values early.
5. **Accountability**: Since the supervisor is accountable for output, delegation requires shared values.

### Assess Task-Relevant Maturity (TRM)

TRM is specific to the task, not general competence:

1. **Definition**: Combination of achievement orientation, readiness for responsibility, education, training, and experience.
2. **Assess Specificity**: A person can have high TRM in one job and low in another.
3. **Identify Low TRM Causes**: New environment, content, tasks, accelerated pace, or abrupt job changes.
4. **Distinguish from General Competence**: High general maturity does not guarantee high TRM in a new role.

## Delegate Tasks

Follow these delegation rules:

1. **Prerequisite Check**: Ensure delegator and delegatee share a common information base and operational ideas.
2. **Avoid Insincere Delegation**: Do not emotionally hold onto tasks while pretending to delegate.
3. **Task Selection**: Delegate activities familiar to you rather than unfamiliar onesâ€”easier to monitor.
4. **Responsibility Principle**: "Delegation without follow-through is abdication." You remain responsible for the outcome.

## Monitor Delegated Tasks

Apply quality assurance monitoring principles:

1. **Timing**: Monitor at the lowest-added-value stage (e.g., review rough drafts, not final polished versions).
2. **Sampling**: Do not check 100% of details. Check randomly just enough to ensure satisfactory progress.
3. **Frequency Based on TRM**:
   - Increase monitoring for newly delegated tasks
   - Decrease intensity as subordinate's work improves
   - Adjust based on specific task maturity, not general ability

## Avoid Meddling

Monitoring ensures the activity proceeds in line with expectations. Meddling is prescribing specific activities. Focus on outcomes and quality, not methods.
