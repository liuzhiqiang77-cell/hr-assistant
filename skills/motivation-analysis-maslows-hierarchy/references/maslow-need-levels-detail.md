# Maslow's Need Levels - Detailed Reference

## Physiological Needs
- **What it is**: Food, water, shelter, clothing
- **Money role**: Direct purchase of necessities
- **Motivation pattern**: Ceases once basic standard met
- **Historical context**: Dominant in pre-industrial and early industrial eras
- **Modern relevance**: Still relevant in developing economies or for lowest-paid workers

## Safety/Security Needs
- **What it is**: Protection from harm, job security, insurance, savings
- **Money role**: Protection against catastrophic loss
- **Motivation pattern**: Rarely dominant, but anxiety if absent
- **Organizational examples**: Pension plans, health insurance, tenure systems
- **Note**: Often taken for granted until threatened

## Social/Affiliation Needs
- **What it is**: Belonging, friendship, love, team identity
- **Money role**: Indirect (enables social activities)
- **Motivation pattern**: Persistent throughout life
- **Organizational examples**: Team building, company events, mentorship programs
- **Key insight**: People seek others like themselves ("birds of a feather")

## Esteem/Recognition Needs
- **What it is**: Status, recognition, respect from others, achievement
- **Money role**: Symbol of achievement/status
- **Motivation pattern**: Self-limiting (goal reached = need satisfied)
- **Organizational examples**: Promotions, titles, awards, corner offices
- **Critical insight**: Recognition must come from respected reference group

## Self-Actualization
- **What it is**: Reaching full potential, personal growth, mastery
- **Money role**: Scorecard of achievement, not end itself
- **Motivation pattern**: Never extinguishes; drives to higher levels
- **Organizational examples**: Challenging assignments, autonomy, mastery opportunities
- **Key characteristic**: Only need that grows when satisfied
