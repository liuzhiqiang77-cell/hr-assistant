# Task-Relevant Maturity-Based Delegation

## When to Use
- You need to delegate tasks to free up time
- You want to develop subordinate capabilities
- You must ensure accountability for delegated work
- A subordinate is taking on new responsibilities
- You need to determine appropriate supervision levels

## Core Concepts

### Task-Relevant Maturity (TRM)
TRM is NOT general maturity or competence. It is specific to the task at hand and combines:
- Achievement orientation (drive to achieve)
- Readiness to take responsibility
- Education, training, and experience relevant to the specific task

A person can have high TRM in one job and low TRM in another. TRM can change day by day or hour by hour based on pace, environment, or task changes.

### Operational Values
Effective delegation requires a shared understanding of:
- How the organization works together
- Priorities within the organization
- The correct way to do things

Without shared operational values, you cannot effectively delegate because you remain accountable for subordinate output.

## Delegation Process

### Step 1: Establish Shared Operational Values
Before delegating:
1. Ensure commonality of operational values, priorities, and preferences
2. Teach the subordinate the correct operational values early
3. Prevent "customer tuition"â€”do not let subordinates learn through customer-impacting mistakes
4. Remember: The supervisor is accountable for teaching values, not the subordinate

### Step 2: Assess TRM
Evaluate the subordinate's TRM for the specific task:
- **Low TRM**: New to task, new environment, or accelerated pace
- **Medium/Growing TRM**: Gaining experience and confidence
- **High TRM**: Experienced veteran with the specific task

### Step 3: Select Tasks to Delegate
Follow these rules:
1. **Ensure common information base**: Delegator and delegatee must share context
2. **Delegate familiar tasks**: Choose tasks you know well (easier to monitor)
3. **Avoid insincere delegation**: Don't emotionally hold onto tasks while pretending to delegate
4. **Retain responsibility**: "Delegation without follow-through is abdication"

### Step 4: Monitor Based on TRM
Apply quality assurance principles:

**Timing Principle**: Monitor at the lowest-added-value stage
- Review rough drafts, not final polished versions
- Catch basic content problems early before expensive work

**Sampling Principle**: Do not check 100% of details
- Go into details randomly just enough to ensure satisfactory progress
- Avoid meddling (prescribing specific activities)

**Frequency Adjustment**: Vary based on TRM
- **Low TRM**: Monitor frequently and intensively
- **Growing TRM**: Decrease monitoring as work improves
- **High TRM**: Minimal monitoring, focus on results

## Key Principles

1. **Supervisor Accountability**: You are accountable for subordinate output, so you cannot delegate without shared values
2. **TRM Specificity**: Always assess TRM for the specific task, not general competence
3. **Internalization**: As TRM grows, structure moves from externally imposed to internally adopted
4. **Monitoring vs. Meddling**: Monitor progress and quality, but do not prescribe specific activities

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- Letting subordinates "pay tuition" via customer-impacting mistakes
- Delegating without a common information base
- Holding onto tasks emotionally while pretending to delegate
- Monitoring 100% of details (meddling)
- Confusing general maturity with task-relevant maturity
