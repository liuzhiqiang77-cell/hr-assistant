# Competitive Performance Management - Examples

## Building Maintenance Competition
**Scenario**: Facility management across multiple buildings

**Implementation**:
- Appoint a "building czar" for each location
- Create scoring rubric for maintenance quality
- Weekly comparison of scores across buildings
- Public posting of rankings

**Result**: Immediate improvement in maintenance quality without monetary incentives

---

## Sales Team Competition
**Scenario**: Regional sales teams

**Implementation**:
- Clear monthly sales targets
- Real-time leaderboard visible to all
- Weekly "game plan" meetings (coach approach)
- Post-game analysis of wins and losses

**Coach Behaviors**:
- Manager takes no credit for team wins
- Provides tough feedback on lost deals
- Focuses on skill development, not just results

**Result**: Increased sales performance and team engagement

---

## Software Development Sprint Competition
**Scenario**: Agile development teams

**Implementation**:
- Story points completed as score
- Velocity tracking across teams
- Retrospectives as "post-game analysis"
- Technical debt reduction as defensive stats

**Coach Behaviors**:
- Code reviews as coaching sessions
- Celebrating team achievements, not manager's
- Normalizing failed sprints as learning opportunities

**Result**: Higher velocity and better code quality

---

## Signs of Destructive Competition
**Warning Indicators**:
- Sabotaging other teams' work
- Hoarding information
- Unethical behavior to win
- Personal attacks between competitors
- Short-term focus at expense of long-term health

**Intervention Strategies**:
- Re-establish rules of fair play
- Emphasize cooperative elements within competition
- Address unethical behavior immediately
- Refocus on personal best vs. beating others
