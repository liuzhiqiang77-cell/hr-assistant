# Self-Actualization: The Unique Motivation Source

## Comparison to Other Needs

| Need Type | Extinguishes After Fulfillment? | Motivation Pattern |
|-----------|----------------------------------|-------------------|
| Physiological | Yes | Stops once basic needs met |
| Safety | Yes | Stops once security achieved |
| Social | Yes | Stops once belonging achieved |
| Esteem | Yes | Stops once status attained (self-limiting) |
| Self-Actualization | **NO** | Continues driving higher performance |

## The Boundary of Capability

Achievement-driven individuals work at the "boundary of their capability." This means:
- They are consistently challenged
- Success is not guaranteed
- Failure is a possibility but not a deterrent
- Each success pushes the boundary outward

Example: A rock climber attempting routes just beyond their current ability level. They fall sometimes (failure), but each attempt improves their skill (growth).

# MBO System Details

## Goal Difficulty Calibration

The "fifty-fifty" rule:
- Too easy: No motivation, no growth
- Too hard: Demotivating, leads to withdrawal
- Just right (50% success): Maximum effort, peak output

## Measuring Output vs Knowledge

**Output Examples**: Products shipped, code deployed, designs completed, revenue generated
**Knowledge Examples**: Theories known, concepts understood, skills acquired (without application)
