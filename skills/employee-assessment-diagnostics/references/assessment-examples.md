# Assessment Examples - Detailed Reference

## Money Motivation Test Examples

### Example 1: Caribbean Plant Workers
**Scenario**: Workers given specific savings goal
**Reaction**: Focus on absolute amount needed
**Result**: Quit immediately after reaching goal
**Diagnosis**: Physiological/Safety needs dominant
**Lesson**: Motivation ceases when utilitarian target met

### Example 2: Venture Capitalists
**Scenario**: Multiple deals, varying compensation
**Reaction**: Focus on relative ranking and deal size
**Result**: Continue seeking larger deals
**Diagnosis**: Esteem/Self-Actualization dominant
**Lesson**: Money as scorecard, boundless motivation

### Example 3: Corporate Manager Raise
**Scenario**: Receives $10,000 raise
**Reaction A**: "Great, I can pay off my car loan"
**Diagnosis**: Safety need dominant
**Implication**: Motivation will decrease after loan paid

**Reaction B**: "But did Jones get more?"
**Diagnosis**: Esteem need dominant
**Implication**: Motivation continues, focused on relative status

### Example 4: Sales Commission
**Scenario**: Commission structure change
**Reaction A**: "This affects my ability to pay mortgage"
**Diagnosis**: Safety need dominant
**Management**: Ensure base salary meets security needs

**Reaction B**: "This changes how I rank against top performers"
**Diagnosis**: Esteem/Self-Actualization dominant
**Management**: Ensure ranking system is visible and meaningful

## Black Box Assessment Weighting Examples

### Scenario 1: Crisis Management
**Context**: Department facing immediate crisis
**Weighting**: Output 90%, Internal 10%
**Rationale**: Current results critical, future concerns secondary
**Measures**: Crisis resolved, immediate output metrics

### Scenario 2: Team Building Phase
**Context**: New team, need long-term capability
**Weighting**: Output 30%, Internal 70%
**Rationale**: Building foundation for future performance
**Measures**: Team cohesion, skill development, process establishment

### Scenario 3: Steady State Operations
**Context**: Established team, routine operations
**Weighting**: Output 60%, Internal 40%
**Rationale**: Balance current results with sustainability
**Measures**: Output metrics, employee development, process improvement

### Scenario 4: Turnaround Situation
**Context**: Failing department needs quick fix
**Weighting**: Output 80%, Internal 20%
**Rationale**: Survival depends on immediate results
**Measures**: Critical output metrics, stabilization activities

## Trade-off Analysis Examples

### Example 1: Sales Manager
**Output Measures**: Exceeded quota by 15%
**Internal Measures**: High team turnover, poor morale
**Trade-off**: Excellent short-term results vs long-term team health
**Assessment**: Good output performance, concerning internal measures
**Action**: Address team issues while recognizing sales success

### Example 2: Engineering Lead
**Output Measures**: Missed deadline by 2 weeks
**Internal Measures**: Excellent mentoring, improved team processes
**Trade-off**: Short-term delay vs long-term capability building
**Assessment**: Concerning output, excellent internal contributions
**Action**: Recognize process improvements, address timeline management

### Example 3: Project Manager
**Output Measures**: On time, on budget
**Internal Measures**: Burned out team, no documentation
**Trade-off**: Project success vs team sustainability
**Assessment**: Good output, poor internal measures
**Action**: Recognize project completion, address team health and documentation

### Example 4: HR Manager
**Output Measures**: Difficult to quantify directly
**Internal Measures**: Excellent employee relations, improved retention
**Trade-off**: Hard-to-measure output vs clear internal contributions
**Assessment**: Strong internal measures, output inferred from retention metrics
**Action**: Focus on retention as proxy for output success
