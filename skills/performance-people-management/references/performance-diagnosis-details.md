# Employee Performance Diagnosis - Detailed Guide

## Pre-Diagnosis Checklist

Before diagnosing performance issues, ensure:
- [ ] Role and expectations are clearly defined
- [ ] Employee understands what success looks like
- [ ] Performance standards are objective and measurable
- [ ] Employee has necessary resources and tools
- [ ] Performance gap is real, not perception

## Diagnostic Questions

### For "Can't Do" (Capability) Issues

1. **Skill Assessment**
   - Does the employee have the required technical skills?
   - Have they performed this task successfully before?
   - Do they understand the process or methodology?

2. **Knowledge Assessment**
   - Do they have the necessary information?
   - Have they been trained on this task?
   - Do they understand why this task matters?

3. **Resource Assessment**
   - Do they have access to required tools?
   - Do they have sufficient time?
   - Are there obstacles preventing success?

4. **Ability Assessment**
   - Is the task within their capability range?
   - Are there physical or mental limitations?
   - Is the role mismatched with their aptitude?

### For "Won't Do" (Motivation) Issues

1. **Value Assessment**
   - Does the employee see value in this task?
   - Do they understand how it contributes to goals?
   - Do they care about the outcome?

2. **Incentive Assessment**
   - Is there positive reinforcement for good performance?
   - Are there consequences for poor performance?
   - Is the reward system aligned with desired behavior?

3. **Confidence Assessment**
   - Does the employee believe they can succeed?
   - Are they afraid of failure?
   - Is there psychological safety?

4. **Obstacle Assessment**
   - Are there personal issues affecting motivation?
   - Is there conflict with team members or manager?
   - Are there external distractions?

## Intervention Strategies

### For "Can't Do" - Training Approaches

**1. Direct Training**
- Formal courses or workshops
- On-the-job training
- Certification programs

**2. Coaching and Mentoring**
- One-on-one guidance
- Shadowing experienced performers
- Regular feedback sessions

**3. Resource Provision**
- Better tools and equipment
- Access to information and documentation
- Improved processes and workflows

**4. Job Redesign**
- Adjust responsibilities to match capabilities
- Provide support structures
- Break complex tasks into simpler steps

### For "Won't Do" - Motivation Approaches

**1. Positive Reinforcement**
- Recognition and praise
- Rewards and incentives
- Career advancement opportunities

**2. Negative Consequences**
- Performance improvement plans
- Clear accountability
- Progressive discipline

**3. Inspiration and Purpose**
- Connect work to larger mission
- Show impact of their contributions
- Create meaning and significance

**4. Relationship Building**
- Understand personal motivations
- Build trust and rapport
- Address personal concerns

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Misdiagnosis
- **Symptom**: Training doesn't improve performance
- **Cause**: Diagnosed as "can't" when actually "won't"
- **Fix**: Reassess motivation factors

### Mistake 2: One-Size-Fits-All
- **Symptom**: Standard approach doesn't work for everyone
- **Cause**: Not individualizing diagnosis and intervention
- **Fix**: Tailor approach to individual circumstances

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Context
- **Symptom**: Performance issues persist despite intervention
- **Cause**: External factors not addressed
- **Fix**: Consider organizational and environmental factors

### Mistake 4: Premature Conclusion
- **Symptom**: Employee labeled as "problem performer"
- **Cause**: Insufficient diagnosis before action
- **Fix**: Thorough assessment before intervention

## Follow-Up Protocol

1. **Set Clear Expectations**
   - Define what improvement looks like
   - Establish timeline for change
   - Agree on measurement criteria

2. **Document Everything**
   - Record performance issues
   - Document interventions
   - Track progress over time

3. **Regular Check-Ins**
   - Schedule frequent feedback sessions
   - Adjust approach as needed
   - Celebrate progress

4. **Escalation Path**
   - Define when to involve HR
   - Establish performance improvement plan criteria
   - Plan for termination if improvement doesn't occur
