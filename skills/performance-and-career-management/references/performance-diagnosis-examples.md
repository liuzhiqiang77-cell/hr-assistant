# Employee Performance Diagnosis: Examples

## Example 1: The Struggling Salesperson

**Situation**: Salesperson consistently missing quota

**Observation**:
- Making calls but not closing deals
- Seems discouraged
- Previously successful, now declining

**Diagnosis Process**:
- **Can't do?** Check if they lack sales skills
  - Assessment: They have strong sales skills, demonstrated success in past
  - Conclusion: Not a capability issue
- **Won't do?** Check motivation
  - Assessment: Recently lost major client, feeling defeated
  - Conclusion: Motivation issue

**Intervention**: Motivation
- Acknowledge the loss and provide support
- Set smaller, achievable goals to rebuild confidence
- Provide incentive for quick wins
- Result: Performance improved

## Example 2: The New Developer

**Situation**: Developer not completing tasks on time

**Observation**:
- Working long hours but not producing
- Seems frustrated
- New to the technology stack

**Diagnosis Process**:
- **Can't do?** Check if they lack technical skills
  - Assessment: Not familiar with company's technology stack
  - Conclusion: Capability issue
- **Won't do?** Check motivation
  - Assessment: Highly motivated, eager to learn
  - Conclusion: Not a motivation issue

**Intervention**: Training
- Provide structured training on technology stack
- Pair with experienced developer for mentoring
- Allow extra time for learning curve
- Result: Performance improved after training period

## Example 3: The Disengaged Manager

**Situation**: Manager not leading team effectively

**Observation**:
- Missing team meetings
- Not providing direction
- Team performance declining

**Diagnosis Process**:
- **Can't do?** Check if they lack management skills
  - Assessment: Previously successful manager, has skills
  - Conclusion: Not a capability issue
- **Won't do?** Check motivation
  - Assessment: Recently passed over for promotion, feeling undervalued
  - Conclusion: Motivation issue

**Intervention**: Motivation
- Have candid conversation about career concerns
- Clarify path for advancement
- Recognize contributions publicly
- Result: Engagement and performance improved

## Example 4: The Mismatched Hire

**Situation**: Employee not performing despite interventions

**Observation**:
- Received training but still not performing
- Motivated and trying hard
- Not meeting expectations despite effort

**Diagnosis Process**:
- **Can't do?** Check capability
  - Assessment: Lacks aptitude for this type of work
  - Conclusion: Capability issue
- **Won't do?** Check motivation
  - Assessment: Highly motivated, wants to succeed
  - Conclusion: Not a motivation issue

**Intervention**: Training attempted but unsuccessful
- Additional training provided
- Coaching and mentoring provided
- Still not meeting expectations

**Resolution**: Role mismatch
- Employee not suited for this role
- Consider transfer to different role
- If no suitable role exists, separation may be necessary

## Diagnostic Framework

### Can't Do Indicators
- Lacks necessary skills or knowledge
- New to role or technology
- Struggles despite effort
- Performance improves with training
- Asks for help frequently

### Won't Do Indicators
- Has skills but doesn't apply them
- Previously successful, now declining
- Performance improves with motivation/incentives
- Disengaged or discouraged
- Effort is low

### Mixed Situations
Sometimes both factors are present:

**Example**: Employee lacking skills AND discouraged
- **Intervention**: Address both
  - Provide training for capability
  - Provide motivation for engagement
  - Monitor which factor is primary barrier

## Manager's Toolkit

### For Capability Issues (Can't Do)
- Formal training programs
- On-the-job coaching
- Mentoring relationships
- Job shadowing
- Structured learning plans
- Time for skill development

### For Motivation Issues (Won't Do)
- Recognition and praise
- Incentives and rewards
- Career development opportunities
- Autonomy and empowerment
- Meaningful work assignments
- Addressing personal or professional barriers

### When Neither Works
- Reassess role fit
- Consider transfer to different position
- Provide honest feedback about fit
- Plan for transition if necessary
- Document performance issues
