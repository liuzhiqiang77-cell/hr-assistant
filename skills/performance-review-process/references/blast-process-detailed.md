# The 'Blast' Performance Review Process - Detailed Guide

## Emotional vs Intellectual Steps

The text emphasizes a critical distinction:

- **Assume Responsibility**: Emotional step (hard)
  - Subordinate realizes: "If it is my problem, I have to do something about it"
  - Implies work and unpleasantness
  - Requires emotional acceptance

- **Find Solution**: Intellectual step (easier)
  - Once responsibility is assumed, finding solutions is straightforward
  - Becomes a shared task between manager and subordinate

## Stage-Specific Techniques

### Stage 1: Ignore
**Signs**: Subordinate doesn't acknowledge the problem exists.

**Techniques**:
- Present concrete data and examples
- Show impact on team/organization
- Use objective metrics to demonstrate reality

### Stage 2: Deny
**Signs**: Subordinate actively argues the problem doesn't exist.

**Techniques**:
- Bring additional evidence and documentation
- Use peer comparisons (carefully)
- Present customer feedback or external validation

### Stage 3: Blame Others
**Signs**: Subordinate admits problem exists but attributes it to external factors.

**Techniques**:
- Acknowledge external factors but focus on response
- Ask: "Given these constraints, what could you have done differently?"
- If stuck, use fallback procedure with position power

### Stage 4: Assume Responsibility
**Signs**: Subordinate says "It's my problem" or "I need to fix this."

**Techniques**:
- Validate and reinforce this step
- Move quickly to solution-finding
- Avoid dwelling on past failures

### Stage 5: Find Solution
**Signs**: Subordinate proposes actions or accepts suggested solutions.

**Techniques**:
- Collaborate on specific, measurable action plans
- Set timelines and check-in points
- Document commitments clearly

## Fallback Procedure Script

> "I understand that you do not see it my way. You may be right or I may be right. However, I am not only empowered, I am required by the organization for which we both work to give you instructions, and this is what I want you to do."

## Monitoring After Fallback

- Schedule regular check-ins
- Document performance against commitment
- If performance doesn't improve, begin formal performance improvement process
- Maintain objective records throughout
