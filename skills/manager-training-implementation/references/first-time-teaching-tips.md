# First-Time Teaching Tips

## Preparation

**Know Your Audience:**
- What do they already know?
- What do they struggle with?
- What questions will they likely ask?

**Start Simple:**
- Don't try to cover everything
- Focus on core concepts
- Build complexity gradually

**Practice Out Loud:**
- Saying it reveals gaps
- Timing becomes clear
- Improves flow

## During Delivery

**Admit When You Don't Know:**
- "That's a great question, I don't know the answer"
- "Let me research that and get back to you"
- Honesty builds credibility

**Use Examples:**
- Real-world scenarios
- Analogies
- Make it concrete

**Check Understanding:**
- Ask questions
- Watch for confusion
- Pause for questions

## After Delivery

**Review What Went Wrong:**
- Where did you stumble?
- What confused people?
- What took too long?

**Celebrate What Went Right:**
- Moments of clarity
- Good questions
- Engagement

**Plan Next Iteration:**
- What will you change?
- What will you keep?
- When will you teach again?

## Emotional Management

**Before:**
- Nervousness is normal
- Preparation reduces anxiety
- Remember: you know more than they think

**During:**
- Focus on helping them learn
- Don't worry about being perfect
- Energy and enthusiasm matter more than polish

**After:**
- You will feel exhausted
- You will also feel exhilarated
- Both are normal and good
