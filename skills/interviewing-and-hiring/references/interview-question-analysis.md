# Interview Question Analysis Framework

## Question Categories and What They Reveal

### Technical/Skills Questions
**'Describe some projects that were highly regarded by your management...'**
- Reveals: Actual impact and recognition received
- Watch for: Specifics vs. generalities, level of recognition

**'What are your weaknesses? How are you working to eliminate them?'**
- Reveals: Self-awareness, commitment to improvement
- Watch for: Honest weaknesses vs. fake strengths ('I work too hard')

### Operational Values Questions
**'Convince me why my company should hire you.'**
- Reveals: Understanding of role, persuasion skills, confidence
- Watch for: Research done on company, alignment with needs

**'Why do you think you're ready for this new job?'**
- Reveals: Career progression logic, self-assessment accuracy
- Watch for: Evidence of readiness vs. overconfidence

### Problem-Solving Questions
**'What are some of the problems you are encountering in your current position? How are you going about solving them? What could you have done to prevent them?'**
- Reveals: Problem identification, solution approach, learning orientation
- Watch for: Blame vs. responsibility, prevention mindset

### Achievement Questions
**'What do you consider your most significant achievements? Why were they important to you?'**
- Reveals: Values, motivation, definition of success
- Watch for: Team vs. individual credit, business impact

## Hypothetical Scenario Examples

### For Manufacturing Role
- Walk through production process from raw material to finished good
- Ask: 'How would you handle a quality issue discovered at final inspection?'
- Calculate: 'What's the finished cost of this wafer given these parameters?'

### For Management Role
- Scenario: 'Your team is missing deadline. Key engineer is sick. What do you do?'
- Observe: Prioritization, communication, problem-solving approach

### For Sales Role
- Scenario: 'Customer objects to price. Competitor is 10% cheaper. How do you respond?'
- Observe: Value articulation, negotiation skills, product knowledge

## Reference Check Best Practices

### Opening the Call
- State purpose clearly
- Mention time commitment needed
- Establish professional context

### Building Rapport
- Find common connections
- Reference shared experiences
- Share relevant context about role

### Key Questions for References
- 'What were [candidate]'s primary responsibilities?'
- 'What would you say were [candidate]'s greatest strengths?'
- 'What areas did [candidate] need to develop?'
- 'How did [candidate] handle pressure or deadlines?'
- 'Would you hire [candidate] again? Why or why not?'
- 'Is there anything else I should know?'

### Red Flags in References
- Hesitation or pauses before answering
- Vague or non-specific praise
- Refusal to comment on certain areas
- Qualified endorsements ('good, but...')
- Inability to provide specific examples
