# Detailed Interview Questions Reference

## Technical/Skills Questions

### Project Experience
- "Describe some projects that were highly regarded by your management, especially by the levels above your immediate supervisor."

### Self-Assessment
- "What are your weaknesses? How are you working to eliminate them?"
- "How good are you technically?"

## What They Did With Knowledge

### Achievements
- "What do you consider your most significant achievements? Why were they important to you?"

### Failures and Learning
- "What could you have done to prevent problems from cropping up?"

## Discrepancies

### Problem Solving
- "What are some of the problems you are encountering in your current position? How are you going about solving them?"

## Operational Values

### Readiness
- "Why do you think you're ready for this new job?"

### Persuasion
- "Convince me why my company should hire you."

## Hypothetical Scenarios

### Process Walkthrough
- Walk the candidate through a specific process relevant to the job (e.g., manufacturing steps)

### Problem Solving
- Ask them to solve a specific problem or calculate a result (e.g., finished cost of a wafer)

## Candidate Questions Analysis

When the candidate asks questions, analyze:
- What they already know about the company/industry
- What they value in a workplace
- How prepared they are for the interview

## Reference Call Structure

### Opening (First 5-10 minutes)
- Introduce yourself and purpose
- Establish common ground if possible

### Information Gathering (Middle 10-15 minutes)
- Ask about the candidate's past performance
- Seek specific examples

### Deep Dive (Last 10 minutes)
- Most valuable time for candid information
- Ask follow-up questions based on rapport built
- Seek any concerns or reservations
