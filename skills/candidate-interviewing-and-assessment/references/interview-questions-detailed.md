# Detailed Interview Question Bank

## Technical/Skills Assessment

### Project Experience
- "Describe some projects that were highly regarded by your management, especially by the levels above your immediate supervisor."
- "What technical challenges have you overcome in your recent projects?"
- "Walk me through your most complex technical achievement."

### Weaknesses and Development
- "What are your weaknesses? How are you working to eliminate them?"
- "What skill gaps have you identified in yourself, and what's your plan to address them?"
- "Tell me about a time you failed to meet a technical expectation. What did you learn?"

### Direct Capability Questions
- "How good are you technically?" (Observe confidence and specificity)
- "Rate yourself on [specific skill] from 1-10 and explain your rating."
- "What would your previous manager say about your technical strengths?"

## Application of Knowledge

### Achievements
- "What do you consider your most significant achievements? Why were they important to you?"
- "Describe a time your work had a measurable business impact."
- "What's the most valuable contribution you've made to your current team?"

### Failures and Learning
- "Tell me about a project that didn't go as planned. What happened?"
- "What could you have done to prevent problems from cropping up?"
- "How do you ensure you learn from mistakes?"

## Operational Values and Fit

### Motivation
- "Convince me why my company should hire you."
- "Why do you think you're ready for this new job?"
- "What attracts you to this role specifically?"

### Problem-Solving Approach
- "What are some of the problems you are encountering in your current position? How are you going about solving them?"
- "Describe your approach to troubleshooting complex issues."

## Hypothetical Scenarios

### Process Walkthrough
- Walk the candidate through a specific process relevant to the job
- Example for manufacturing: "Describe the complete process from raw material receipt to finished product shipment. Where are the critical control points?"

### Problem-Solving Exercises
- Ask them to solve a specific problem or calculate a result
- Example: "Given these parameters, calculate the finished cost of a wafer and explain your methodology."
- Assess their ability to derive principles on the fly

### Situational Questions
- "How would you handle [specific scenario relevant to the role]?"
- "What would you do if [challenging situation] occurred?"

## Candidate Questions Analysis

When the candidate asks questions, analyze:

### What They Already Know
- Depth of questions indicates research level
- Specific questions about company operations suggest preparation

### What They Value
- Questions about culture indicate values alignment
- Questions about growth indicate career ambition
- Questions about team dynamics indicate collaboration focus

### Preparation Level
- Well-researched questions show genuine interest
- Generic questions may indicate lack of preparation
- Strategic questions indicate business acumen
