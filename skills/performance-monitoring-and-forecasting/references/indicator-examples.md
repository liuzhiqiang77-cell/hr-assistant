# Detailed Indicator Examples

## Linearity Indicator: College Recruiting

### Scenario
- **Target**: 100 acceptances by June
- **Timeline**: September to June (10 months)
- **Ideal Line**: 10 acceptances per month

### Progress Tracking

| Month | Target (Cumulative) | Actual | Variance | Required Rate |
|-------|---------------------|--------|----------|---------------|
| Sep | 10 | 8 | -2 | 11.1/month |
| Oct | 20 | 15 | -5 | 10.6/month |
| Nov | 30 | 18 | -12 | 12.9/month |
| Dec | 40 | 25 | -15 | 12.5/month |

### Analysis
- By November, significantly behind target
- Must increase rate from 10/month to ~13/month
- Early warning allows intervention before it's too late

## Paired Indicators: Real-World Examples

### Inventory Management
- **Primary**: Inventory value ($500,000)
- **Counter**: Stockouts per month (12)
- **Risk**: Driving inventory too low causes shortages
- **Balance**: Find optimal level minimizing total cost

### Software Development
- **Primary**: Features delivered per sprint (15)
- **Counter**: Bugs found in QA (8)
- **Risk**: Rushing features increases bug count
- **Balance**: Maintain quality while delivering features

### Sales Team
- **Primary**: Revenue per month ($1M)
- **Counter**: Customer returns/complaints (5%)
- **Risk**: Aggressive selling damages customer satisfaction
- **Balance**: Sustainable growth with happy customers
