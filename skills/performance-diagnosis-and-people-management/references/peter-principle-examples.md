# Peter Principle: Common Scenarios

## Scenario 1: The Star Salesperson
- **Current Role**: Top-performing sales representative
- **Promoted to**: Sales Manager
- **Problem**: Skills for selling (persuasion, persistence) differ from skills for managing (coaching, planning, delegation)
- **Result**: Former star struggles as manager, reaches level of incompetence

## Scenario 2: The Technical Expert
- **Current Role**: Brilliant software engineer
- **Promoted to**: Engineering Manager
- **Problem**: Technical excellence doesn't translate to people management
- **Result**: Loses technical contribution while failing at management

## Scenario 3: The Individual Contributor
- **Current Role**: High-performing analyst
- **Promoted to**: Team Lead
- **Problem**: Individual work habits don't scale to team coordination
- **Result**: Becomes bottleneck, team performance suffers

## Prevention Checklist
- [ ] Have we identified the specific competencies required for the new role?
- [ ] Do these competencies differ from the current role's competencies?
- [ ] Has the candidate demonstrated these new competencies?
- [ ] Have we provided training to bridge any skill gaps?
- [ ] Is there a trial period to test fit before permanent promotion?
