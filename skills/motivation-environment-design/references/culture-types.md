# Organizational Culture Types: Output vs Knowledge

## Output-Centered Culture

### Definition
Culture that values and emphasizes tangible results and measurable performance.

### Key Characteristics
- Tangible results are highly valued and esteemed
- Performance is the primary measure of worth
- "What have you produced lately?" is the key question
- Recognition tied to outcomes, not expertise
- Abstract knowledge without application gets little recognition

### Behaviors and Norms
- Celebrating completed projects and shipped products
- Promoting based on track record, not potential
- Meetings focus on decisions and actions, not theories
- Success stories highlight results achieved
- Failure analyzed for learning, not excused

### Management Practices
- Clear performance metrics
- Results-based compensation
- Regular performance reviews tied to output
- Public recognition of achievements
- Promotion based on demonstrated results

### Advantages
- High actual achievement
- Clear accountability
- Focus on what matters to business
- Self-reinforcing motivation

### Examples
- Sales organizations with clear quotas
- Manufacturing with production targets
- Startups focused on shipping
- Consulting firms based on deliverables

## Knowledge-Centered Culture

### Definition
Culture that values expertise, credentials, and abstract knowledge over tangible results.

### Key Characteristics
- Knowing abstractly is valued over producing results
- Expertise and credentials are primary status markers
- "What do you know?" is the key question
- Recognition tied to position and credentials, not output
- Theoretical knowledge valued even without application

### Behaviors and Norms
- Long discussions about concepts and theories
- Credential collecting (degrees, certifications)
- Meetings as intellectual exercises
- Success stories highlight expertise and insights
- Failure explained with sophisticated rationalizations

### Management Practices
- Promotion based on credentials and tenure
- Recognition for expertise and knowledge
- Compensation tied to position, not performance
- Emphasis on "thought leadership" over execution
- Tolerance for lack of tangible results

### Disadvantages
- Low actual achievement
- Lack of accountability
- Focus on appearance over substance
- Motivation problems for achievers

### Examples
- Academic institutions (in extreme form)
- Bureaucracies with rigid hierarchies
- Consulting firms focused on "smarts" over delivery
- R&D organizations disconnected from market

---

## Transitioning from Knowledge to Output Culture

### Steps
1. **Redefine success metrics** - Measure output, not activity
2. **Change recognition patterns** - Celebrate results, not expertise
3. **Adjust compensation** - Tie rewards to performance
4. **Modify meeting norms** - Focus on decisions and actions
5. **Update promotion criteria** - Base advancement on track record

### Challenges
- Resistance from knowledge-centered incumbents
- Difficulty measuring some types of output
- Cultural inertia
- Fear of losing status

### Leadership Requirements
- Model output-focused behavior
- Make tough decisions on non-performers
- Consistently reward results
- Communicate why the change matters
