# Self-Actualization Driver Profiles

## Competence-Driven Profile

### Definition
Focus on achieving mastery and excellence in a specific domain or skill.

### Behavioral Indicators
- Seeks opportunities to practice and refine skills
- Compares current performance to personal best
- Invests time in skill development without immediate reward
- Shows frustration with own errors, not external factors
- Seeks feedback for improvement, not validation

### Examples
- **Musician:** Practices scales daily for years to achieve technical perfection
- **Craftsman:** Spends extra time perfecting details invisible to others
- **Athlete:** Trains obsessively to shave fractions of seconds off time
- **Programmer:** Refactors code for elegance when it already works

### Management Strategies
- Provide opportunities for skill development
- Assign progressively challenging tasks in same domain
- Offer technical mentorship and expert feedback
- Recognize mastery and excellence
- Allow time for deliberate practice

### Motivation Triggers
- "Can I do this better than before?"
- "What's the most excellent way to accomplish this?"
- "How can I master this skill?"

## Achievement-Driven Profile

### Definition
Abstract need to achieve and succeed across endeavors, not tied to specific domain.

### Behavioral Indicators
- Seeks challenges at "boundary of capability"
- Takes calculated risks (not reckless, not conservative)
- Measures success against external standards
- Moves to new challenges after achieving goals
- Competitive with self and others

### The Peg/Ring Experiment Classification

**Gamblers:**
- High risk, no influence on outcome
- Prefer chance over skill
- Not true achievers

**Conservatives:**
- Very little risk
- Prefer guaranteed success
- Avoid challenge

**Achievers:**
- Challenge themselves at boundary of capability
- Prefer 50% success probability
- Test limits of ability

### Examples
- **Entrepreneur:** Starts new venture after selling previous one
- **Executive:** Takes on turnaround assignment after successful growth role
- **Salesperson:** Consistently targets quota slightly above reach
- **Researcher:** Tackles problems with unknown solutions

### Management Strategies
- Set stretch goals with 50% success probability
- Provide new challenges after current goals achieved
- Create competitive environment with clear metrics
- Recognize achievement and winning
- Allow failure as part of growth process

### Motivation Triggers
- "Can I achieve this difficult goal?"
- "What's the next challenge?"
- "How far can I push myself?"

---

## Common Characteristics

Both profiles share:
- Spontaneous drive to test outer limits of ability
- Self-sustaining motivation (doesn't extinguish)
- Focus on performance, not external rewards
- Resistance to routine, unchallenging work
- Need for autonomy in pursuit of goals

## Management Differences

| Aspect | Competence-Driven | Achievement-Driven |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| Focus | Skill mastery | Goal achievement |
| Time Horizon | Long-term development | Medium-term targets |
| Comparison | Self vs. past self | Self vs. external standard |
| Ideal Assignment | Deep expertise role | Challenging project |
| Recognition | Excellence in craft | Winning/achieving |
